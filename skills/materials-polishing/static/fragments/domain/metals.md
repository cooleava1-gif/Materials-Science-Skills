# Metals and Alloys — Polishing Guide

## Terminology to verify

| Term | Domain-specific check |
|---|---|
| grain refinement | Requires quantitative grain size (linear intercept per ASTM E112) |
| strengthening | Specify mechanism: Hall-Petch, precipitation, solid solution, work hardening |
| ductility | Report elongation % or reduction of area, not just "good ductility" |
| fracture mode | Requires fractography (SEM), not inferred from tensile curve shape |
| heat treatment | Must include temperature + time + cooling rate (most critical parameter) |

## Common overclaim patterns

| Overclaim | Safer alternative |
|---|---|
| "A novel alloy was developed" | "A [composition] alloy with [X%] improvement in [property] was produced" |
| "The material exhibits superior strength" | "UTS reached [value] MPa, a [X%] increase over [reference]" |
| "Grain refinement was achieved" | "Average grain size decreased from [A] to [B] µm after [processing]" |
| "Excellent corrosion resistance" | "Corrosion rate of [value] mm/year in [test] under [condition]" |

## Hedging guide

| Evidence level | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Quantitative EBSD/TEM + statistics | indicates, demonstrates | "EBSD indicates a [X]% increase in high-angle grain boundaries" |
| SEM + literature comparison | suggests, is consistent with | "The fracture surface suggests ductile failure with dimple formation" |
| Mechanical data without microstructure | may be attributed to | "The strength increase may be attributed to grain refinement based on Hall-Petch relationship" |

## Journal-specific language

- Report mechanical properties with standard test methods (ASTM E8/E21, ISO 6892).
- Specify specimen orientation: "longitudinal (L), transverse (T), or short-transverse (S-T)".
- "High strength" is relative: compare with a known grade (e.g., "comparable to 6061-T6").

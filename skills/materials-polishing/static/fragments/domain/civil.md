# Civil / Construction Materials — Polishing Guide

## Terminology to verify

| Term | Domain-specific check |
|---|---|
| curing curing | Emulsion curing ≠ cement hydration ≠ epoxy crosslinking — specify mechanism |
| strength development | Report age/condition: "7-day compressive strength", "28-day moist curing" |
| workability | Slump, flow, or viscosity? Specify test method |
| durability | Define: freeze-thaw? chloride? aging? moisture? Each has different test |
| optimum content | Must show full dosage range, not just the peak |

## Common overclaim patterns

| Overclaim | Safer alternative |
|---|---|
| "The modified binder exhibits excellent field performance" | "Bond strength improved by X% under laboratory conditions" |
| "The optimum epoxy content is 12%" | "Within the tested range, 12% epoxy gave the highest bonding strength" |
| "FTIR confirms the curing reaction" | "FTIR shows decreased epoxide peak at 915 cm⁻¹, consistent with curing" |
| "This material is environmentally friendly" | Replace with specific metric (e.g., "X% reduction in carbon footprint vs conventional") |

## Hedging guide

| Evidence level | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Direct evidence (FTIR peak + kinetic data) | indicates, demonstrates | "The FTIR peak decrease indicates curing progression" |
| Supporting evidence (SEM alone) | suggests, is consistent with | "The morphology suggests improved compatibility" |
| Indirect evidence (performance only) | may be attributed to, could reflect | "The strength increase may be attributed to improved adhesion" |

## Journal-specific language (civil)

- **CBM / Construction and Building Materials**: Prefer practical significance + standard test methods. Avoid "excellent" without comparison.
- **CCC / Cement and Concrete Composites**: Hydration chemistry must be precise. "C-S-H" not "CSH". Report w/c ratio exactly.
- **RMPD / Road Materials and Pavement Design**: Emphasize pavement engineering context. Report test temperature for binder tests.

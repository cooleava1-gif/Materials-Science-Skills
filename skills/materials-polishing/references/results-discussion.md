# Results and Discussion Polishing

> **Domain context**: The `domain` axis has loaded domain-specific polishing guidance for [detected domain]. The domain guide contains domain-specific evidence ladder expectations and trade-off patterns.

Use evidence ladders:

data trend -> comparison with control -> mechanism evidence -> service implication -> limitation.

Avoid writing every result as positive. Trade-offs are often the most publishable part of materials work.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Quantified but bounded trend (civil)

Before:
> The bonding strength was greatly improved after adding waterborne epoxy, proving that the modifier has excellent performance.

After:
> The modified emulsion showed a higher mean pull-off strength than the control under the tested curing condition. This result supports a performance benefit, but the dosage range and replicate variability should be reported before claiming an optimum formulation.

### Example 2: Trade-off discussion (polymers)

Before:
> The viscosity increased with epoxy dosage, which shows the material is better.

After:
> Increasing epoxy dosage raised viscosity, which may improve film build-up but can also reduce sprayability and field workability. The discussion should therefore connect viscosity to both bonding performance and construction constraints.

### Example 3: Mechanism qualification (ceramics)

Before:
> The high strength was caused by the dense structure.

After:
> The sample sintered at 1600°C reached 98.5% relative density, and its flexural strength of 450 MPa was significantly higher than samples sintered at 1500°C (92% density, 320 MPa). The density-strength correlation is consistent with the expected porosity dependence of brittle ceramic strength, though grain size effects cannot be excluded without microstructural analysis.

### Example 4: Performance with boundary (functional)

Before:
> The device shows excellent efficiency and stability.

After:
> The champion device achieved a PCE of 19.8% under AM 1.5G illumination (active area 0.09 cm²), retaining 85% of initial efficiency after 500 h of continuous operation at maximum power point under 1-sun illumination at 25°C. Device-to-device variation across 20 devices was ±1.2%.

### Example 5: Structure-property link (metals)

Before:
> The grain refinement made the alloy stronger.

After:
> After thermomechanical processing, the average grain size decreased from 45 to 12 µm, corresponding to an increase in yield strength from 280 to 410 MPa — consistent with a Hall-Petch contribution of approximately 120 MPa. EBSD confirmed a higher fraction of high-angle grain boundaries after processing.

# Asphalt and Pavement Language

Use asphalt/pavement-specific terminology:

- penetration grade, not "hardness grade" for asphalt,
- softening point (ring and ball), not "melting point",
- emulsified asphalt, not "liquid asphalt",
- demulsification or breaking, not "drying" for emulsion setting,
- tack coat, not "adhesive layer",
- interlayer bond strength, not "adhesion strength" without test method,
- cohesive failure / adhesive failure, not "failure mode" without classification,
- moisture damage or stripping, not "water damage",
- rutting resistance or dynamic stability, not "deformation resistance",
- fatigue life (cycles to failure), not "durability" without data,
- elastic recovery, not "rebound" for modified asphalt,
- storage stability (upper/lower softening point difference), not "stability" without measurement,
- residue or residue properties, not "dried asphalt" for emulsion residue.

Do not describe emulsion curing only as "drying" without distinguishing demulsification, moisture loss, and strength gain stages. Report curing condition (time, temperature, substrate) before claiming bonding performance.

Avoid generic "polymer-modified material" framing when the target is RMPD, IJPE, JRE, or CBM in Transportation.

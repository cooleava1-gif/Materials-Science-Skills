# Nano Language

Use nanomaterial-specific terminology:

- nanoparticle size or hydrodynamic diameter, not "particle size" without method qualifier,
- polydispersity index (PDI), not "size variation",
- surface area (BET), not "surface area" without measurement method,
- zeta potential, not "surface charge" in formal text,
- agglomeration / aggregation, not "clumping" (and distinguish primary particle vs agglomerate),
- exfoliation, not "peeling" for layered materials,
- aspect ratio, not "length-to-width ratio",
- quantum confinement, not "size effect" for optical properties,
- percolation threshold, not "critical loading" for electrical/thermal conductivity,
- grafting density (chains/nm²), not "surface coverage" without quantification,
- nanocomposite, not "nano-filled material",
- thin film thickness, not "coating thickness" for deposited layers,
- sheet resistance (Ω/sq), not "resistivity" for thin films.

Do not describe nanoparticles only as "nano-sized" without reporting size distribution (PDI or histogram) and measurement method (TEM, DLS, XRD). Report agglomeration state at working concentration, not just as-synthesized dry state.

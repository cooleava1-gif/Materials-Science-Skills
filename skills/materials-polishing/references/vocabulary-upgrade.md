# Vocabulary Upgrade Table

Use this table to replace weak, vague, or imprecise words with
evidence-calibrated alternatives. Every replacement must preserve the original
meaning while matching the language strength to the evidence level.

---

## 1. Evidence-Calibrated Verbs

The most important vocabulary choice in a materials paper is the **verb**.
It must match the strength of the evidence.

### Verb ladder

| Evidence level | Safe verbs | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct measurement** | showed, exhibited, displayed, reached, attained, achieved, recorded, measured | You have the data in front of you |
| **Replicated trend** | demonstrated, confirmed, established, verified, validated | Multiple replicates or methods agree |
| **Mechanism inference** | suggested, indicated, pointed to, implied, was consistent with | Single method or indirect evidence |
| **Speculation** | may reflect, could arise from, appears to, seems likely, might be explained by | Beyond direct observation |
| **Engineering implication** | has potential to, is expected to, shows promise for, may improve | No field data yet |
| **Durability claim** | maintained X after Y exposure, retained X% after Y | Tested under specific condition |
| **Comparison** | outperformed, was higher than, was lower than, exceeded, was comparable to | Direct comparison exists |

### Common violations

| Wrong | Why wrong | Correct |
|---|---|---|
| `The results prove that...` | Proof requires definitive evidence | `The results show that...` |
| `FTIR confirmed the mechanism` | FTIR alone cannot confirm a mechanism | `FTIR results are consistent with...` |
| `The material is excellent` | Subjective, no criterion | Remove, or state measured value |
| `This significantly improved...` | "Significantly" requires statistics | `This increased...` (or add p-value) |
| `The mechanism is crosslinking` | Stated as fact without sufficient evidence | `Crosslinking is suggested by...` |

## 2. Weak → Strong Replacements

### Adjectives

| Weak / vague | Stronger / precise | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `good` | `higher`, `improved`, `within the target range` | Always quantify if possible |
| `better` | `higher than the control by X%` | State the comparison |
| `excellent` | Remove, or state measured criterion | Subjective |
| `nice` | Remove | Not academic |
| `huge` | `substantial` (only with data) | Hyperbolic |
| `important` | `critical`, `essential`, or state why | Vague |
| `significant` | Only with p-value or statistical test | Misleading without stats |
| `remarkable` | Remove or quantify | Hyperbolic |
| `interesting` | Remove | Adds nothing |
| `novel` | `to our knowledge, not previously reported` | Requires literature check |
| `unique` | `distinct`, `different from` | "Unique" is absolute |
| `perfect` | Remove or qualify | Not achievable |
| `optimal` | `optimal within the tested range` | May not be globally optimal |

### Adverbs

| Weak / vague | Stronger / precise | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `very` | Remove (usually adds nothing) | Intensifier without content |
| `really` | Remove | Not academic |
| `quite` | Remove or replace with `somewhat` | Vague degree |
| `extremely` | Remove or quantify | Hyperbolic |
| `greatly` | `substantially`, `by X%` | Quantify if possible |
| `obviously` | Remove | Condescending |
| `clearly` | Remove | Asserts without evidence |
| `basically` | Remove | Filler |
| `actually` | Remove | Filler |
| `just` | Remove | Informal |

### Phrases

| Weak / vague | Stronger / precise |
|---|---|
| `a lot of` | `many`, `numerous`, or quantify |
| `in order to` | `to` |
| `due to the fact that` | `because` |
| `it is worth noting that` | Delete; state the point directly |
| `it should be mentioned that` | Delete; state the point directly |
| `as a matter of fact` | Remove |
| `in the event that` | `if` |
| `with regard to` | `regarding`, `for` |
| `at this point in time` | `now`, `currently` |
| `the vast majority of` | `most` |
| `a large number of` | `many` |

## 3. Materials-Specific Vocabulary

### Performance descriptions

| Vague | Precise |
|---|---|
| `The performance was good` | `The bond strength reached 0.78 MPa` |
| `The results were better` | `The modified group showed 73% higher bond strength than the control` |
| `The material has good properties` | `The material showed higher tensile strength and comparable flexural modulus` |
| `The modification improved the material` | `WER modification increased bond strength from 0.45 to 0.78 MPa` |

### Mechanism language

| Overstated | Calibrated |
|---|---|
| `The mechanism is crosslinking` | `Crosslinking is suggested by the disappearance of the 915 cm⁻¹ epoxide peak` |
| `The chemical bonding mechanism was confirmed` | `FTIR results are consistent with chemical bonding between WER and asphalt` |
| `The morphology proves phase separation` | `SEM images show a co-continuous morphology consistent with phase separation` |
| `The curing reaction completely finished` | `The epoxide peak decreased substantially after 7 d curing` |

### Durability language

| Overstated | Calibrated |
|---|---|
| `The material has excellent durability` | `The material retained 85% bond strength after 5 freeze-thaw cycles` |
| `Long-term performance was guaranteed` | `The 28-d strength was maintained after 5 freeze-thaw cycles` |
| `The material is resistant to aging` | `The modified group showed 12% lower strength loss after RTFO aging than the control` |
| `Field performance was validated` | `Field testing under controlled conditions showed...` (only if field data exist) |

### Sustainability language

| Overstated | Calibrated |
|---|---|
| `The material is green` | Remove, or state specific environmental benefit with evidence |
| `Environmentally friendly` | `May reduce environmental burden within the assessed boundary` |
| `Sustainable material` | Only with LCA, emission, or resource-bound evidence |
| `Low-carbon alternative` | Only with carbon footprint data and comparison boundary |
| `Recycled material` | `Material containing X% recycled content` (state source and processing) |

## 4. Hedging Patterns

Use hedging to calibrate claim strength. Each pattern below has a safe and
unsafe form.

| Unsafe | Safe |
|---|---|
| `This proves that...` | `This suggests that...` |
| `The improvement is caused by...` | `The improvement may be associated with...` |
| `In general, WER improves...` | `Within the investigated dosage range, WER increased...` |
| `Under all conditions...` | `Under the tested curing condition...` |
| `Compared with all materials...` | `Compared with the control group...` |
| `This is the first study to...` | `To our knowledge, this is the first study to...` |
| `The material will perform well in the field` | `The material shows promise for field application` |
| `This completely solves the problem` | `This addresses one aspect of the problem` |

---

## 5. Upgrade Workflow

When editing a sentence:

1. **Identify the claim type**: measured result, mechanism inference, or
   engineering implication.
2. **Check the evidence level**: direct measurement, replicated trend,
   single method, or speculation.
3. **Choose a verb from the matching row** in the verb ladder.
4. **Replace weak adjectives/adverbs** with precise alternatives.
5. **Add hedging** if the evidence level is moderate or speculative.
6. **Verify the sentence** still says what the author intended.

### Example

Before:
> The waterborne epoxy significantly improved the bonding strength and the
> material showed excellent performance, proving that the modification has
> great potential for engineering applications.

After:
> Waterborne epoxy increased the mean bond strength from 0.45 to 0.78 MPa
> (ASTM D7234, 7 d curing). This improvement is consistent with epoxy
> crosslinking and suggests potential for pavement interlayer bonding, subject
> to field validation.

Changes:
- `significantly improved` → `increased` (no statistics provided)
- `excellent performance` → `mean bond strength from 0.45 to 0.78 MPa`
- `proving` → `consistent with` (FTIR evidence, not proof)
- `great potential for engineering applications` → `potential for pavement
  interlayer bonding, subject to field validation`

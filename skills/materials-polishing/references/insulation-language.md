# Thermal Insulation Language

Use insulation-specific terminology:

- thermal conductivity (λ or k), not "heat transfer coefficient",
- thermal resistance (R-value), not "insulation value",
- heat flux, not "heat flow" in formal measurement context,
- steady-state measurement (hot plate, heat flow meter), not "test" without method,
- transient measurement (hot wire, laser flash), not "quick test",
- aerogel, not "air gel" or "nanofoam",
- superinsulation, not "ultra insulation" (use only when λ < 0.020 W/m·K),
- porosity (open/closed), not "air content",
- pore size distribution (BET/MIP), not "pore structure" without measurement,
- hydrothermal aging, not "moisture damage" for insulation degradation,
- thermal cycling stability, not "heat resistance" without cycling data,
- flame spread index (FSI), not "fire rating" without specifying standard (ASTM E84, EN 13501),
- smoke development index, not "smoke" without quantification,
- water vapor permeability (perm or ng/Pa·s·m²), not "breathability".

Do not describe aerogel composites only as "porous" without reporting density, pore size, and thermal conductivity as a function of density. Report measurement method and specimen thickness before comparing conductivity values.

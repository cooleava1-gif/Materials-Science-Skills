# Introduction Polishing

> **Domain context**: The `domain` axis has loaded domain-specific polishing guidance for [detected domain]. The introduction arc varies by domain — the domain guide contains domain-specific gap-framing strategies.

The introduction should move from:

engineering problem -> material strategy -> unresolved bottleneck -> study objective -> evidence plan.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: From literature list to gap (civil)

Before:
> Many researchers have studied emulsified asphalt, waterborne epoxy, polyurethane, SBR latex, and other modifiers. These studies show good results.

After:
> Polymer modification has been used to improve the cohesion and adhesion of emulsified asphalt systems, yet the balance between emulsion stability, epoxy curing, viscosity growth, and interface bonding remains insufficiently resolved.

Why: turns a list of materials into a research bottleneck that can motivate the study.

### Example 2: From literature list to gap (metals)

Before:
> Many studies have investigated aluminum alloys. Heat treatment is very important for mechanical properties.

After:
> Age-hardenable 6xxx series aluminum alloys are widely used in automotive applications, but the effect of natural pre-aging on subsequent artificial aging kinetics — and the resulting strength-ductility balance — remains poorly quantified for alloys with high Mg/Si ratios.

Why: specifies the alloy class, application, and a concrete kinetic-structural gap.

### Example 3: Narrowing scope (polymers)

Before:
> Polymer composites have attracted much attention due to their light weight and high strength.

After:
> Short-fiber reinforced polymer composites offer a favorable strength-to-weight ratio for automotive interior components, but the fiber-matrix interface under humid conditions — and its effect on long-term creep behavior — is not well understood for polypropylene-based systems.

Why: replaces generic "attention" with a specific application, material system, and unresolved interface durability question.

### Example 4: Functional materials gap framing

Before:
> Perovskite solar cells have great potential for future energy applications.

After:
> Perovskite solar cells have achieved power conversion efficiencies exceeding 26%, yet their operational stability under combined thermal and illumination stress remains the primary barrier to commercial deployment.

Why: replaces "great potential" with a specific performance benchmark and a named degradation barrier.

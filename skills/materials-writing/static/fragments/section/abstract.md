# Abstract

> **Domain context**: The `domain` axis has loaded domain-specific writing guidance for [detected domain]. The abstract must satisfy both the general structure below and the domain's evidence expectations.

Use the five-part structure:

1. Background — what is known and why it matters.
2. Gap — what is not known or what limits current materials.
3. Method — material design or experimental approach.
4. Result — key quantitative finding.
5. Implication — what the result means, with boundary.

Keep the abstract claim-evidence-boundary safe. A result may say the modified material improved bonding under a specific condition. It should not claim field durability or a complete mechanism unless those data are supplied.

## Domain-specific result reporting

- **Civil**: report the improvement percentage and the test condition (temperature, humidity, aging state).
- **Ceramics**: report density and the key property with processing condition.
- **Metals**: report both strength and ductility/toughness change.
- **Polymers**: report both the target property and the trade-off property.
- **Functional**: report the performance metric with measurement condition.
- **Nanomaterials**: report size/morphology and the size-dependent property.

## Domain checklist

- [ ] Are all quantitative results in the abstract supported by data in the manuscript?
- [ ] Is the test condition or measurement context specified?
- [ ] Are field or commercial claims avoided without supporting evidence?

# Introduction

> **Domain context**: The `domain` axis has loaded domain-specific writing guidance for [detected domain]. The funnel below is the general structure; the domain guide contains domain-specific gap types, citation strategy, and reviewer expectations. Use both together.

Use a funnel:

1. Engineering or scientific context.
2. Material limitation (performance ceiling, processing barrier, durability gap).
3. Current strategies and their limits.
4. Unresolved gap chain — what specific evidence is missing.
5. This study's contribution and approach.

The gap chain must be specific: not "few studies exist", but what evidence is missing, under what condition, and for which material system.

- For **civil materials**: e.g., wet bonding retention, storage stability-performance coupling, mechanism linking modification to interface bonding.
- For **ceramics**: e.g., sintering temperature-microstructure trade-off, missing Weibull statistics, phase stability under service conditions.
- For **metals**: e.g., strength-ductility synergy unexplored for this composition, corrosion-fatigue interaction unknown, precipitate evolution during thermal exposure.
- For **polymers**: e.g., the effect of crosslink density on creep behavior, thermal-mechanical trade-off in composite formulation.
- For **functional materials**: e.g., the efficiency-stability gap in device operation, role of defect density in transport properties.
- For **nanomaterials**: e.g., the link between size distribution and catalytic selectivity, aggregation behavior in realistic media.

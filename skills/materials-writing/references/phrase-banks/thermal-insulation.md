# Thermal Insulation Phrase Bank

Use this bank for building insulation, aerogel, foam, porous ceramic, and
composite thermal insulation manuscripts.

## phrase-bank

### thermal conductivity

- `The thermal conductivity decreased from [value] to [value] W m-1 K-1 as [pore former/filler/density] changed from [level] to [level].`
- `The reduction in thermal conductivity is consistent with [porosity/pore-size distribution/radiative barrier], but the contribution of [moisture/convection] requires separate verification.`

### density

- `At a density of [value] kg m-3, [material] achieved [thermal conductivity] while retaining [mechanical/fire/durability metric].`
- `The performance should be compared at similar density because density strongly affects both thermal conductivity and compressive behavior.`

### pore structure

- `SEM or image analysis shows [closed/open/interconnected] pore structure with [pore size range], which helps explain [thermal/mechanical response].`
- `The pore-structure interpretation is supported by [SEM/MIP/micro-CT], not only by bulk density changes.`

### hygrothermal aging

- `After [hygrothermal aging protocol], thermal conductivity changed by [value] and [mechanical metric] retained [percentage], indicating [bounded durability claim].`
- `Moisture uptake may increase heat transfer through [pore water/solid bridge], so dry-state thermal conductivity cannot be used as the only service indicator.`

### fire performance

- `The [LOI/cone calorimetry/non-combustibility] result indicates improved fire performance under [standard], but smoke/toxicity behavior remains outside the present evidence.`
- `The insulation window is defensible only when thermal conductivity, density, moisture resistance, and fire performance are considered together.`

## reviewer-red-flags

- Comparing thermal conductivity across samples with very different density without normalization or discussion.
- Claiming service insulation performance from dry-state conductivity alone.
- Explaining heat-transfer mechanisms without pore-structure evidence.
- Mentioning fire safety without standard, flame exposure, heat release, or classification basis.

## safe-claim-patterns

- `Within the tested density range, [material] shows a trade-off between thermal conductivity and [mechanical/fire/durability metric].`
- `[Pore evidence] supports the interpretation that [pore feature] contributes to lower thermal conductivity under dry-state conditions.`
- `The application window is limited to [temperature/humidity/fire standard] until long-term hygrothermal aging and fire classification are verified.`

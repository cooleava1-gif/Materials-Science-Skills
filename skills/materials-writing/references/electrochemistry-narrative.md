# Electrochemistry Narrative Guide

## Research Paper Types

### Electrode Material Development
- Novel anode/cathode material synthesis and characterization
- Capacity, rate capability, and cycling stability evaluation
- Structure-performance relationship

### Electrolyte Engineering
- Liquid/solid/gel electrolyte design
- Ionic conductivity and electrochemical stability window
- Interface compatibility with electrodes

### Mechanism Study
- Charge storage mechanism (intercalation, conversion, alloying)
- Kinetics analysis (b-value, diffusion/contribution ratio)
- Degradation mechanism identification

## Standard Manuscript Structure

### Introduction
1. Energy storage context and application requirements
2. Current material limitations (capacity, stability, rate)
3. Strategy and novelty of proposed approach
4. Expected performance metrics

### Experimental
- Material synthesis (precursor, method, conditions)
- Electrode preparation (mass loading, binder, current collector)
- Cell assembly (coin cell type, electrolyte composition)
- Electrochemical testing parameters (voltage window, C-rate definition)

### Results and Discussion
1. Material characterization (XRD, SEM, TEM, XPS)
2. Electrochemical performance (CV, GCD, EIS)
3. Rate capability and cycling stability
4. Mechanism analysis and performance correlation
5. Comparison with literature benchmarks

### Conclusion
- Key performance achievements with numbers
- Mechanism insight
- Practical implications and future outlook

## Key Metrics to Report

| Metric | Minimum Requirement | Best Practice |
|---|---|---|
| Mass loading | Reported (mg/cm²) | > 2 mg/cm² for practical relevance |
| Capacity | At representative cycle | At least 5 cycles at each C-rate |
| Cycling stability | > 100 cycles | > 500 for Li-ion, > 10000 for supercapacitors |
| Coulombic efficiency | Reported | > 99% after initial cycles |
| Error bars | 3 replicate cells | Mean ± SD for all key metrics |
| EIS | Rct reported | Full Nyquist with equivalent circuit |

## Common Figure Types

1. **CV curves** — at multiple scan rates for kinetics analysis
2. **GCD profiles** — at different current densities
3. **Rate capability** — capacity at increasing C-rates
4. **Cycling stability** — capacity and efficiency vs cycle number
5. **EIS Nyquist** — before/after cycling, with equivalent circuit
6. **Ragone plot** — energy density vs power density comparison

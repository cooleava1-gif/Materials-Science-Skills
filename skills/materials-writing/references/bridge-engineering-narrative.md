# Bridge Engineering Narrative Guide

## Research Paper Types

### Structural Assessment and Load Rating
- Field load testing with deflection/strain measurement
- Load rating analysis per AASHTO LRFR or Eurocode
- Finite element model calibration and validation

### Structural Health Monitoring
- Sensor system design and optimization
- Damage detection algorithms
- Long-term monitoring data analysis

### Deterioration and Remaining Life
- Chloride-induced corrosion modeling
- Concrete carbonation and alkali-silica reaction
- Fatigue life estimation for steel bridges

### Rehabilitation and Strengthening
- CFRP/GFRP strengthening design
- External post-tensioning
- Deck replacement and overlay strategies

## Standard Manuscript Structure

### Introduction
1. Bridge infrastructure context (network condition, aging stock)
2. Specific structural problem (load deficiency, deterioration, damage)
3. Study objective (assessment method, monitoring system, repair strategy)

### Methods
- Bridge description (type, span, age, materials, traffic)
- Testing/monitoring protocol (instruments, load cases, duration)
- Analytical method (FEM, code-based, reliability)
- Validation approach

### Results and Discussion
1. Current condition and measured response
2. Model predictions vs field measurements
3. Parametric study or sensitivity analysis
4. Comparison with code requirements
5. Remaining life or retrofit effectiveness

### Conclusion
- Assessment outcome with quantitative metrics
- Recommended action with engineering justification
- Limitations and future work

## Key Metrics to Report

| Metric | Unit | Minimum Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Deflection | mm | At midspan and quarter points under known loads |
| Strain | microstrain | At critical sections with temperature compensation |
| Natural frequency | Hz | First 3 modes minimum |
| Damping ratio | % | From free vibration or ambient vibration |
| Rating factor | — | Inventory and operating per AASHTO LRFR |
| Condition state | 1–4 | Per NBI or element-level condition state |

# Highway Engineering Narrative Guide

## Research Paper Types

### Pavement Structural Analysis
- Mechanistic-empirical design methods
- Layer modulus back-calculation from deflection data
- Stress-strain analysis under traffic loading

### Performance Evaluation and Prediction
- Distress progression modeling (rutting, cracking, roughness)
- Remaining service life estimation
- Performance-based specifications

### Maintenance and Rehabilitation
- Treatment selection and timing optimization
- Overlay design methods
- Life-cycle cost analysis (LCCA)

### Pavement Materials Characterization
- Mix design optimization
- Field performance correlation
- Accelerated pavement testing

## Standard Manuscript Structure

### Introduction
1. Highway infrastructure context and economic significance
2. Current design/evaluation limitations
3. Study objective and scope (road class, climate, traffic)

### Methods
- Pavement structure (layer types, thicknesses, materials)
- Testing protocol (FWD, IRI, rutting, PCI)
- Data collection period and frequency
- Analysis methodology (empirical, mechanistic, or hybrid)

### Results and Discussion
1. Current pavement condition assessment
2. Distress trends and progression rates
3. Performance prediction model calibration/validation
4. Strategy comparison with cost-effectiveness
5. Sensitivity analysis (traffic, climate, material variability)

### Conclusion
- Key findings with quantitative performance metrics
- Recommended strategy with justification
- Implementation guidance and limitations

## Key Metrics to Report

| Metric | Unit | Minimum Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Rutting depth | mm | Measured at wheel path, average of both lanes |
| IRI | m/km | Profile measured at 100m intervals minimum |
| PCI | 0–100 | ASTM D6433 distress survey |
| Deflection | 0.01 mm | FWD at standard 40 kN load |
| Structural number | — | AASHTO 1993 or MEPDG |
| Traffic (ESALs) | million | With growth rate and design period |

## Common Figure Types

1. **Distress progression curves** — rutting, cracking, IRI vs pavement age
2. **Deflection basin** — FWD sensor data with distance from load
3. **Pavement cross-section** — layer structure diagram
4. **Traffic loading spectrum** — axle load distribution
5. **Performance comparison** — treatment strategies side by side
6. **Cost-effectiveness** — agency cost vs performance benefit

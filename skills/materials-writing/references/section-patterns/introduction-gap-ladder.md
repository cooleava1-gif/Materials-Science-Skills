# Introduction Gap Ladder

Use this section-level factory to build a materials introduction that narrows
from field progress to a testable entry point.

## Move 1: field progress

Purpose: establish what the field can already do.

Sentence pattern:

- `Recent studies on [material family] have improved [property/application] through [dominant strategy].`
- `For [application], [material/system] has attracted attention because it can [documented advantage].`

## Move 2: unresolved contradiction

Purpose: show the tension that keeps the problem scientifically alive.

Sentence pattern:

- `However, improvements in [target property] often coincide with losses in [competing property], creating a contradiction between [performance goal A] and [goal B].`
- `Although [strategy] enhances [metric], its influence on [durability/interface/processability] remains inconsistent across [test conditions].`

## Move 3: evidence gap

Purpose: identify what evidence is missing, not merely what topic is missing.

Sentence pattern:

- `The current evidence is limited by [missing characterization/test standard/sample condition], making it difficult to separate [mechanism A] from [alternative explanation].`
- `Few studies report [property] together with [mechanism evidence], so the structure-property link remains weak for [material context].`

## Move 4: paper entry

Purpose: define exactly how this paper enters the gap.

Sentence pattern:

- `Therefore, this study investigates [material variable] using [test matrix] and [characterization method] to clarify [bounded mechanism/property relation].`
- `The objective is to determine whether [hypothesis] can explain [measured response] within [composition/process/service boundary].`

## Gap ladder quality check

The ladder is weak if the final entry could apply to any material. Anchor the
entry to a specific property, mechanism evidence, and service condition.

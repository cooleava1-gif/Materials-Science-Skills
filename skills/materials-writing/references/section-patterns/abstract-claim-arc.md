# Abstract Claim Arc

Use this section-level factory for materials science abstracts. Keep the arc in
this order so the abstract moves from field need to evidence-bounded impact.

## Move 1: background pain

Purpose: name the material problem, not the whole field.

Sentence pattern:

- `[Material/system] is increasingly used for [application], but [performance bottleneck] still limits [service condition or engineering use].`
- `For [material family], the practical challenge is not only [target property], but also [durability/processability/interface constraint].`

## Move 2: method

Purpose: state the design or test strategy with enough specificity to be
reproducible at abstract level.

Sentence pattern:

- `Here, we prepared [material variant] by [processing route] and evaluated [property set] under [standard/condition].`
- `We combined [performance test] with [characterization method] to link [structure/mechanism feature] to [engineering property].`

## Move 3: key result

Purpose: give the strongest quantitative result without inflating causality.

Sentence pattern:

- `Compared with [control], [optimized formulation] increased [property] by [value] while maintaining [secondary constraint].`
- `The lowest/highest [metric] was obtained at [composition/process window], indicating a trade-off between [property A] and [property B].`

## Move 4: mechanism explanation

Purpose: connect the result to supported mechanism evidence.

Sentence pattern:

- `[Characterization evidence] suggests that [observed structural/chemical feature] is associated with [mechanism], which helps explain [performance change].`
- `The result is consistent with [mechanism evidence], rather than direct proof of [unsupported molecular/field-scale process].`

## Move 5: application boundary

Purpose: close with the engineering boundary, not a universal claim.

Sentence pattern:

- `These results identify [composition/process window] as a promising route for [application] under [tested condition], while [unresolved durability/scale-up issue] remains to be verified.`
- `The proposed system is most defensible for [service scenario], because [evidence basis] was limited to [test standard/sample scale].`

## Anti-overclaim check

Do not write: `[Material] proves a new mechanism for all [application].`

Write instead: `[Evidence] supports a mechanism in which [bounded explanation] under the tested [composition/condition] range.`

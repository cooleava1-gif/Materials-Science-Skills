# Review Synthesis Patterns

Use this section-level factory for materials review papers, mini-reviews, and
evidence maps.

## Pattern 1: classification

Purpose: classify by mechanism or material design, not by a loose list.

Sentence pattern:

- `Existing studies can be grouped into [class A], [class B], and [class C] according to [mechanism/property/process criterion].`
- `This classification separates [material systems] by [evidence-bearing feature], which is more informative than grouping only by [application].`

## Pattern 2: controversy

Purpose: explain why studies disagree.

Sentence pattern:

- `The reported effect of [variable] remains controversial because studies differ in [test standard], [sample preparation], and [service condition].`
- `One group attributes [property change] to [mechanism A], whereas another emphasizes [mechanism B]; the distinction is difficult because [missing evidence].`

## Pattern 3: trend

Purpose: show directional movement in the field.

Sentence pattern:

- `The literature is shifting from single-property optimization toward coupled evaluation of [property], [durability], and [mechanism evidence].`
- `Recent work increasingly combines [characterization method] with [performance test], enabling stronger process-structure-property interpretation.`

## Pattern 4: research gap

Purpose: frame the gap as missing evidence or missing boundary conditions.

Sentence pattern:

- `The key research gap is not the absence of [topic], but the lack of [evidence type] linking [mechanism] to [engineering performance].`
- `Future studies should report [standard/sample preparation/durability protocol] so that [material systems] can be compared across [application boundary].`

## Review synthesis boundary

Do not convert a literature trend into a factual mechanism unless the reviewed
papers supply consistent characterization support.

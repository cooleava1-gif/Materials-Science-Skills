# ACS Nano — Formatting Facts

Authoritative facts about ACS Nano formatting requirements. This file holds the **facts**; each skill's `static/fragments/journal/acs-nano.md` adds the **action layer**.

> Always verify against the journal's current Guide for Authors before final submission. The numbers below are accurate as of early 2026.
> Official page: https://pubs.acs.org/page/ancac3/submission/authors.html

## Article types and limits

| Article type | Word limit | Abstract | References | Figures/Tables | Methods |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Article** | ~6,000 | ~200 words, unstructured | ~40–60 typical | Flexible | End of main text |
| **Letter** | ~3,000 | ~150 words | ~25–30 | ~4–5 | End of main text |
| **Review** | Longer (8,000–15,000) | ~200 words | Comprehensive (100+) | Flexible | N/A |
| **Perspective** | ~4,000 | ~150 words | ~50 | Flexible | N/A |

## Abstract

- Unstructured, concise prose
- ~200 words for Articles; ~150 for Letters
- No citations in abstract
- Must convey the nanoscience/nanotechnology advance clearly
- Include quantitative results where possible

## Figures and tables

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Resolution (halftone/photo) | 300 dpi minimum |
| Resolution (line art) | 1,000 dpi minimum |
| Resolution (combination) | 600 dpi minimum |
| File formats | TIFF, EPS, PDF |
| Color mode | Color online free; ACS charges for print color |
| In-figure font | ≥6 pt at final size |
| Panel labels | Lowercase letters in parentheses: (a), (b), (c) |

## References

- **Style**: ACS style (author-date or numbered depending on format)
- **In-text**: Numbered [1] or author-date (Smith et al. 2025)
- **Format example**:
  ```
  [1] Smith, A. B.; Johnson, C. D. Title of Article. ACS Nano 2025, 19, 1234–1245.
  ```
- Journal names: ACS abbreviated format
- Include DOI; list all authors (no "et al." in reference list)

## Mandatory statements

- **Data Availability statement** — required (ACS policy)
- **Author Contributions** — required (CRediT or equivalent)
- **Conflict of Interest** — required (ACS disclosure form)
- **Acknowledgments** — optional
- **Supporting Information** — encouraged

## Cover letter

ACS Nano editors use the cover letter for initial assessment. Emphasize:

1. The nanoscience/nanotechnology advance (one sentence)
2. Why it matters to the broad nanoscience community
3. Potential applications or cross-disciplinary impact

## Common desk-rejection patterns

1. **Not nano-focused** — work does not address nanoscale phenomena or nanostructures
2. **Incremental synthesis** — minor variation on known nanomaterial preparation
3. **Lacks mechanistic insight** — nanostructure-property relationship not established
4. **Limited scope** — narrow technical advance without broad significance
5. **Insufficient characterization** — nanoscale structure not adequately confirmed

## Open access

- ACS journal; hybrid open access model
- OA APC applies (verify current rate, ACS AuthorChoice)
- Default: subscription access
- ACS Editors' Choice option available

# Cement and Concrete Review Criteria

## Mix design and proportions

Critical checkpoints:

1. Cement type (CEM I, CEM II, etc. or ASTM Type I-V) and source must be reported.
2. Water-binder ratio must be stated explicitly, not just "water content".
3. All SCMs (fly ash, silica fume, slag, metakaolin) must report source, fineness, and chemical composition (SiO2, Al2O3, CaO, LOI).
4. Aggregate: type, size distribution, specific gravity, absorption.
5. Superplasticizer: type (polycarboxylate, naphthalene, melamine), solid content, dosage.

Common weaknesses:

- Cement brand given without type or composition.
- Water-binder ratio not calculated when SCMs are used.
- Aggregate properties not reported.
- Superplasticizer dosage given as "mL" without solid content.

## Fresh properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Workability: slump (ASTM C143 / EN 12350-2) or flow table (ASTM C230) with ambient temperature.
2. Setting time (ASTM C191 / EN 196-3): initial and final, with ambient and specimen temperature.
3. Air content (ASTM C231 / EN 12350-7) for concrete; density for mortar.

Common weaknesses:

- Slump reported without ambient temperature.
- Only one workability measurement at time zero; no time-dependent loss.
- Air content not measured when superplasticizer is used.

## Mechanical properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Compressive strength: specimen size (cube vs cylinder), age (7d, 28d, 56d), number of specimens (min 3), loading rate.
2. Standard must be cited: ASTM C39/C109, EN 196-1, GB/T 17671, or JGJ/T 70.
3. Flexural strength: specimen dimensions, span, loading rate, standard.
4. Elastic modulus: ASTM C469 or equivalent, with stress-strain curve.

Common weaknesses:

- Cube strength reported but journal expects cylinder (or vice versa).
- Only 28-day strength; no early or late age data.
- Strength reported without standard deviation.
- Specimen size not stated.

## Durability

Critical checkpoints:

1. Chloride penetration: ASTM C1202 (RCPT), NT Build 492 (migration), or ASTM C1556 (profile).
2. Carbonation: exposure conditions (CO2 concentration, RH, temperature), depth measurement method.
3. Freeze-thaw: ASTM C666 (A or B), number of cycles, dynamic modulus retention.
4. Sulfate resistance: ASTM C1012, expansion at specified ages.

Common weaknesses:

- Only one durability test performed; "durable" claim too broad.
- Chloride penetration tested at only one age.
- Carbonation depth reported without exposure conditions.
- Freeze-thaw tested without air content measurement.

## Supplementary cementitious materials

Critical checkpoints:

1. Replacement level must be by mass of cement, clearly stated.
2. Physical properties: specific gravity, specific surface area (Blaine or BET), particle size.
3. Chemical composition (XRF) with major oxides and LOI.
4. Activity index (strength activity ratio) at 7d and 28d per ASTM C311 or EN 450-1.

Common weaknesses:

- Replacement level ambiguous (% of cement vs % of binder).
- Chemical composition not reported for SCMs.
- "Green" or "sustainable" claims without LCA or CO2 comparison data.

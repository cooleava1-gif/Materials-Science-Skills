# Semiconductors Review Criteria

## Material growth and structure

Critical checkpoints:

1. Substrate material, orientation, and pre-treatment must be specified.
2. Growth or deposition method (CVD, MBE, MOCVD, sputtering, solution processing) must include temperature, pressure/flow rates, precursor purity, and growth time.
3. Film thickness or layer dimensions must be measured by cross-sectional SEM, TEM, ellipsometry, or profilometry.
4. Crystallinity and phase must be confirmed by XRD, Raman, or TEM; epitaxial relationships reported where relevant.
5. Doping type and concentration must be verified by Hall effect, capacitance-voltage, or secondary-ion mass spectrometry.

Common weaknesses:

- Carrier type inferred from sign of Seebeck coefficient without Hall confirmation.
- Thickness estimated from growth time without direct measurement.
- Phase purity assumed from XRD peak position alone without considering texture or strain.
- Doping concentration reported from nominal precursor ratio rather than measurement.

## Electronic properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Hall measurement must report magnetic field, temperature, sample geometry, and contact configuration.
2. Mobility values must be accompanied by carrier concentration; both compared against established benchmarks for the material.
3. Temperature-dependent transport must be shown to distinguish hopping, band-like, or impurity-scattering mechanisms.
4. Contact resistance must be characterized by transfer-length method or current-voltage symmetry checks.
5. Resistivity measurements must specify two-probe vs. four-probe geometry and electrode material.

Common weaknesses:

- Mobility reported at a single carrier density without context.
- Ohmic behavior assumed from linear I-V over a narrow voltage range.
- Contact-limited transport misattributed to intrinsic material mobility.
- Comparison made across different doping levels or measurement temperatures without normalization.

## Device performance

Critical checkpoints:

1. Device architecture must include layer stack, thicknesses, and active area.
2. For solar cells: J-V curves under AM1.5G with scan direction, scan rate, hysteresis index, and masked aperture area.
3. For transistors: channel length/width, dielectric capacitance, and measurement atmosphere reported.
4. For photodetectors: responsivity, detectivity, response time, and noise bandwidth.
5. Statistical data from at least 5–10 devices; champion values clearly labeled as such.

Common weaknesses:

- Only champion device reported without distribution.
- Active area estimated rather than measured.
- Hysteresis in J-V ignored or not quantified.
- Device measurements performed under unreported atmosphere or humidity.

## Stability and reliability

Critical checkpoints:

1. Stability tests must specify temperature, atmosphere, bias, and duration.
2. Degradation metrics must be tracked continuously or at multiple time points.
3. Encapsulation or passivation methods must be described for ambient testing.
4. For perovskites: moisture, oxygen, and light-soaking conditions reported separately.
5. Mechanistic insight from in-situ characterization or post-mortem analysis preferred.

Common weaknesses:

- Stability claim based on a single final measurement.
- Test duration too short for the target application.
- Multiple stress factors changed simultaneously without isolating effects.
- Degradation mechanism asserted without supporting evidence.

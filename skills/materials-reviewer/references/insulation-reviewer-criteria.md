# Thermal Insulation Review Criteria

## Thermal conductivity measurement

Critical checkpoints:

1. Measurement standard must be reported: ASTM C518 (heat flow meter), ASTM C177 (guarded hot plate), ISO 8301, or ISO 22007-2 (transient).
2. Mean temperature of measurement must be stated; conductivity is temperature-dependent.
3. Temperature gradient across specimen must be reported.
4. Specimen conditioning: temperature, relative humidity, and duration prior to measurement.
5. Number of specimens and measurement replicates.

Common weaknesses:

- "Room temperature" stated without exact temperature.
- No standard cited.
- Single measurement reported without replicate.
- Density not reported alongside conductivity.

## Density and pore structure

Critical checkpoints:

1. Bulk density (mass/volume) and skeletal density (pycnometer) must both be reported for porous materials.
2. Porosity: total, open, and closed fractions should be distinguished.
3. Pore size distribution (mercury intrusion porosimetry, BET, micro-CT) method and sample preparation.

Common weaknesses:

- Only bulk density reported without porosity.
- "Nanoporous" claimed without pore size distribution data.
- Mercury intrusion reported without equilibration time or contact angle assumption.

## Mechanical integrity

Critical checkpoints:

1. Compressive strength must report strain at failure or at specified deformation (10%, 25%).
2. For brittle foams: stress-strain curve with three regions (elastic, plateau, densification).
3. Flexural strength for board/panel insulation: span, specimen width, loading rate (ASTM C203).

Common weaknesses:

- Compressive strength reported without strain or deformation criterion.
- Only one loading direction tested for anisotropic materials.
- Strength reported without density, making comparison meaningless.

## Durability and service conditions

Critical checkpoints:

1. Moisture absorption: equilibrium moisture content at specified RH (50%, 80%, 95%) and temperature.
2. Effect of moisture on thermal conductivity must be quantified, not just stated.
3. Freeze-thaw cycling: number of cycles, temperature range, property retention (conductivity, strength).
4. Hygrothermal aging: temperature, RH, duration, and post-aging properties.

Common weaknesses:

- Thermal conductivity measured dry only, but building application claims made.
- Aging test duration too short for meaningful conclusions.
- Moisture content during conductivity measurement not controlled or reported.

## Fire performance (if claimed)

Critical checkpoints:

1. Reaction to fire class (Euroclass A1-A2, B, C, etc. or national equivalent).
2. Limiting oxygen index (LOI) test standard and specimen dimensions.
3. Cone calorimeter: heat flux, specimen thickness, time to ignition, peak heat release rate.

Common weaknesses:

- "Non-combustible" claimed without test evidence.
- No fire test standard cited.

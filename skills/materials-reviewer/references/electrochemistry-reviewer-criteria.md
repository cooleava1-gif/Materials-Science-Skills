# Electrochemistry Reviewer Criteria

## Top 10 Reviewer Concerns for Battery / Energy Storage Papers

### 1. Mass Loading Not Reported
- Mass loading must be stated for all electrodes
- Low loading (< 1 mg/cm²) is not practically relevant
- Compare at same loading across all samples

### 2. Insufficient Cycling Data
- Claiming "excellent stability" after only 100 cycles is premature
- Li-ion: minimum 500 cycles; supercapacitors: minimum 10000 cycles
- Capacity retention AND coulombic efficiency must be reported together

### 3. No Error Bars / Replicates
- At least 3 replicate cells required
- Report mean ± SD for capacity, efficiency, and resistance
- Do not cherry-pick the best cell

### 4. CV Kinetics Misinterpreted
- "b-value" analysis requires at least 5 scan rates
- Diffusion/contribution separation must show methodology
- Do not claim capacitive behavior from a single CV shape

### 5. EIS Over-Interpreted
- Equivalent circuit must be justified (not just fitted)
- Rct change must be correlated with structural/chemical evidence
- Do not claim "lower charge-transfer resistance" without equivalent circuit

### 6. Rate Capability at Unrealistic Conditions
- Must test at practical C-rates (0.1C to 10C minimum)
- Recovery test at original C-rate after high-rate cycling
- Report capacity retention percentage at each rate

### 7. Comparison Fairness
- Compare with state-of-the-art at same testing conditions
- Include a table of literature comparison with standardized metrics
- Do not compare liquid electrolyte with solid electrolyte data

### 8. Mechanism Claim Without Evidence
- "Synergistic effect" requires direct evidence (XPS, TEM, EIS)
- "Enhanced conductivity" needs EIS data before and after
- "Structural stability" needs post-cycling characterization

### 9. Voltage Window Not Justified
- State voltage window and justify for the chemistry
- Do not use unrealistic voltage windows to inflate capacity
- Report overpotential for supercapacitor devices

### 10. Missing Practical Considerations
- Electrolyte volume, cell type (coin/pouch), and testing temperature
- Calendar aging effects for long-term claims
- Cost and scalability mentioned in Discussion

## Red Flags

- Capacity reported only at first cycle
- No coulombic efficiency data
- Ragone plot without specifying gravimetric/volumetric
- "First report" claim without thorough literature search
- GCD curves with large IR drop ignored

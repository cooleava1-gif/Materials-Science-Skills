# Nanoparticles and Colloidal Nanomaterials Review Criteria

## Synthesis and purification

Critical checkpoints:

1. Precursors, solvents, capping agents, and their purity grades must be listed.
2. Reaction temperature, time, atmosphere, and heating method must be reported.
3. Purification method (precipitation, centrifugation, dialysis) and number of cycles must be described.
4. Yield or concentration must be quantified where synthesis scalability is claimed.
5. Batch-to-batch reproducibility must be demonstrated for size-sensitive properties.

Common weaknesses:

- Synthesis described with missing reagent ratios.
- Purification insufficient to remove unreacted precursors or excess ligands.
- Yield not reported for scale-up claims.
- Single batch used for all reported properties.

## Size and morphology

Critical checkpoints:

1. Size distribution must be based on TEM or AFM statistics with n ≥ 100 particles for near-spherical shapes.
2. Aspect ratio and shape distribution must be reported for anisotropic particles.
3. DLS data must include hydrodynamic diameter and polydispersity index; TEM validation required for non-spherical particles.
4. Aggregation state must be assessed by TEM and DLS under relevant solvent conditions.
5. Surface ligand density must be quantified by thermogravimetric analysis or spectroscopy where relevant.

Common weaknesses:

- Size distribution from a small number of particles or a single micrograph.
- DLS used as the sole technique for anisotropic particles.
- Aggregation ignored despite visible clusters in TEM.
- Ligand shell assumed from synthesis recipe without characterization.

## Optical and electronic properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. UV-Vis absorption must include solvent, concentration, and path length.
2. Photoluminescence must report excitation wavelength, quantum yield method, and stability under illumination.
3. Bandgap or exciton energy must be extracted with appropriate model (Tauc, first-derivative peak).
4. Carrier dynamics must be measured by time-resolved spectroscopy for photonic or photocatalytic claims.
5. Surface trap states must be discussed if photoluminescence is broad or unstable.

Common weaknesses:

- PLQY reported without reference standard or integration sphere.
- Bandgap estimated from absorption onset without model.
- Concentration-dependent aggregation ignored in optical spectra.
- Trap emission misinterpreted as band-edge emission.

## Application performance

Critical checkpoints:

1. Catalytic activity must be normalized to catalyst mass, surface area, or active site count.
2. Sensing response must include selectivity, limit of detection, and response/recovery times.
3. Biomedical studies must report cell line/organism, dose, exposure time, and appropriate controls.
4. Stability under application conditions (pH, ionic strength, light) must be quantified.

Common weaknesses:

- Activity reported without normalization, making comparison impossible.
- Cytotoxicity tested without dose-response or positive controls.
- Stability inferred from a single post-use measurement.

# Journal: Nature Materials

Official page to verify before submission:

- https://www.nature.com/nmat
- https://www.nature.com/nmat/for-authors

## Best fit

Groundbreaking materials science with cross-disciplinary impact, including novel material discovery, transformative synthesis methods, fundamental understanding of material behavior, and materials with implications across physics, chemistry, biology, and engineering.

## Strong manuscript profile

- Conceptual breakthrough or paradigm shift in materials science.
- Results are significant beyond the immediate subfield.
- Mechanistic insight is deep and well-supported.
- Cross-disciplinary relevance is clear.
- Data quality and characterization are exceptional.

## Weak fit

- Incremental improvement on known material systems.
- Narrow scope relevant only to a single subcommunity.
- Applied work without fundamental materials science insight.
- Engineering optimization without new understanding.
- Studies lacking comprehensive characterization or mechanistic depth.

## Nature Materials writing stance

Write for a broad scientific audience. Avoid jargon specific to a single subfield. Frame novelty as a conceptual advance, not just a technical improvement. Emphasize why the finding matters beyond materials science.

## Review caution

Nature Materials does not publish unsolicited reviews. Review articles are typically commissioned by the editors. Do not submit a review without prior arrangement.

# Domain: Functional and Electronic Materials

Use for semiconductors, dielectrics, piezoelectrics, ferroelectrics, photonics, thermoelectrics, superconductors, and energy materials (batteries, supercapacitors, solar cells). Covers synthesis, crystal structure, functional properties, and device-level performance.

## Core logic

Functional materials papers need to connect:

1. Synthesis or deposition method (sol-gel, CVD, PVD, hydrothermal, solid-state).
2. Crystal structure and phase purity.
3. Functional properties (electrical, optical, magnetic, thermal).
4. Device performance or application demonstration.
5. Stability and reliability under operating conditions.

## Key evidence categories

- Structure: XRD (phase, lattice parameters), Raman, FTIR, TEM (SAED, HRTEM).
- Optical: UV-Vis (bandgap, absorption), photoluminescence (PL), ellipsometry.
- Electrical: I-V curves, C-V curves, Hall effect (carrier concentration, mobility), dielectric constant and loss.
- Device: power conversion efficiency (PCE), EQE/IQE, on/off ratio, sensitivity, response time.
- Stability: cyclic testing, accelerated aging, shelf-life, operational stability under bias/light/heat.

## Reviewer risks

- Missing stability testing: excellent initial performance but no degradation data.
- Single-device reporting: one champion device; reproducibility and statistics absent.
- Overclaiming from material to device: bulk material properties assumed to translate to device performance.
- Bandgap extraction misuse: Tauc plot method applied incorrectly or to inappropriate absorption regime.
- Contact effects ignored: Schottky barriers or contact resistance influence measurements but not discussed.
- Operating conditions unrealistic: testing at room temperature/ambient but intended for harsh service.

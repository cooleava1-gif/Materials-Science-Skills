# Domain: Nonferrous Alloys

## Usage

Covers aluminum alloys (2xxx, 5xxx, 6xxx, 7xxx series), copper alloys (brass, bronze,
beryllium-copper), magnesium alloys (AZ, AM, WE series), and zinc alloys. Applicable to
aerospace, automotive, electrical, marine, and lightweight structural applications.

## Core logic

1. Alloying elements and processing route (casting, rolling, extrusion, forging) determine solute supersaturation and texture.
2. Precipitation sequence and aging treatment (natural, artificial, T6, T7x) control precipitate type, size, and distribution.
3. Strength-ductility balance is optimized through precipitate shearing vs. Orowan bypass regime selection.
4. Corrosion resistance (galvanic, pitting, SCC, exfoliation) and wear behavior are validated in application environment.
5. Joining (welding, brazing, adhesive bonding) and surface treatment (anodizing, conversion coating) compatibility close the design loop.

## Key evidence categories

- Mechanical: tensile (RT and temperature range), hardness (Vickers, microhardness profiles), fatigue (S-N, ε-N)
- Microstructural: SEM/TEM (precipitates, grain structure, texture), EBSD (grain orientation, recrystallization fraction)
- Phase: XRD (lattice parameter shifts, precipitate identification), DSC (precipitation dissolution peaks)
- Corrosion: salt spray (ASTM B117), potentiodynamic polarization, intergranular corrosion (ASTM G67 NAMLT)
- Wear: pin-on-disk (coefficient of friction, wear rate), abrasive wear testing
- Thermal: dilatometry (CTE), thermal conductivity (laser flash)

## Reviewer risks

- Aging kinetics (time-temperature-property curves) not reported; single temper condition tested without optimization context.
- Environmental corrosion data limited to salt spray hours; no electrochemical or in-service environment validation.
- Overclaiming from binary model alloy to commercial multi-component alloy without compositional verification.
- Welding or joining effects on HAZ microstructure and properties not characterized.
- Texture and anisotropy ignored; tensile data from single orientation only in wrought products.

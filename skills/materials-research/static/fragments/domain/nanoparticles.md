# Domain: Nanoparticles

Covers metal oxide NPs (TiO2, ZnO, Fe3O4), quantum dots (CdSe, InP, carbon dots), carbon nanotubes, and magnetic nanoparticles for catalysis, imaging, sensing, and biomedical applications.

## Core logic

1. Synthesis method (co-precipitation, hydrothermal, thermal decomposition, sol-gel, hot-injection) controls size, shape, crystallinity, and surface chemistry.
2. Size and morphology must be statistically characterized (≥100 particles, histogram + standard deviation) from TEM; single-image claims are unreliable.
3. Surface functionalization (ligands, polymers, silanes) governs colloidal stability, biocompatibility, and application-specific reactivity.
4. Properties (optical, magnetic, catalytic) are size-dependent; the size-property relationship must be explicitly discussed, not just the final value.
5. Application performance (catalytic activity, MRI relaxivity, drug loading) requires testing in the target medium, not just in ideal solvent.
6. Batch-to-batch reproducibility is a critical scale-up concern; reported properties should include variability data.

## Key evidence categories

- TEM: particle size distribution (histogram), morphology, lattice fringes (HRTEM), SAED
- DLS: hydrodynamic diameter, polydispersity index (PDI) in application-relevant solvent
- XRD: crystal phase, crystallite size (Scherrer/Williamson-Hall)
- BET: specific surface area (especially for catalytic NPs)
- Zeta potential: surface charge and colloidal stability
- UV-Vis/PL: surface plasmon resonance, band edge, emission wavelength
- Magnetic measurements (VSM/SQUID): Ms, Mr, coercivity for magnetic NPs

## Reviewer risks

- Size distribution histogram missing; only "average size ~X nm" stated without spread.
- Aggregation in application medium (biological fluid, reaction solution) ignored; DLS in water only.
- Batch-to-batch reproducibility not reported; one synthesis shown as representative.
- Surface ligand density and identity not quantified; FTIR peak assigned but coverage unknown.
- Catalytic activity reported without turnover number (TON) or comparison to commercial catalyst.

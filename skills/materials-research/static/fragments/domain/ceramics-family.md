# Domain: Ceramics Family

Use for structural ceramics, functional ceramics, refractory materials, bioceramics, glass-ceramics, and ceramic coatings. Covers powder processing, sintering, densification, and performance of ceramic systems.

## Core logic

Ceramics papers need to connect:

1. Powder synthesis or raw material selection.
2. Sintering or densification process (temperature, atmosphere, pressure, time).
3. Microstructure and phase evolution (grain size, porosity, phase composition).
4. Mechanical or functional performance.
5. Reliability and statistical strength distribution.

## Key evidence categories

- Densification: bulk density (Archimedes), open/closed porosity, relative density, shrinkage curve.
- Mechanical: hardness (Vickers/Knoop), fracture toughness (SENB, IF, ISE), flexural strength (3-pt/4-pt bend).
- Phase and structure: XRD (Rietveld refinement), Raman, FTIR.
- Microstructure: SEM (fracture surface, polished section), TEM, EDS, grain size analysis.
- Thermal: TGA/DSC, thermal expansion (dilatometry), thermal conductivity, thermal shock resistance.
- Functional (if applicable): dielectric constant, piezoelectric coefficient, ionic conductivity, biocompatibility.

## Reviewer risks

- Brittle fracture neglect: only strength reported; no toughness or flaw analysis.
- Sintering condition sensitivity: small temperature changes cause large property variation but optimization not shown.
- Missing reliability data: Weibull modulus or statistical strength distribution not reported.
- Grain size effect ignored: mechanism depends on grain size but measurement missing.
- Porosity-performance link unclear: porosity reported but not connected to mechanical or functional behavior.
- Single composition: no compositional gradient or optimization range explored.

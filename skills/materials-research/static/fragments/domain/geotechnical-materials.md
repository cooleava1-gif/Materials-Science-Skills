# Domain: Geotechnical and Geoenvironmental Materials

Use for soils, rocks, grouting materials, stabilized earth, geosynthetics, and ground improvement.

## Core logic

A geotechnical-materials manuscript must define:

- Material origin and classification (soil type, rock type, grout composition, geosynthetic type).
- Ground condition or service exposure (moisture, confining pressure, chemical environment).
- Stabilization or improvement mechanism.
- Test standard and specimen preparation.
- Performance-to-application link (field condition, design parameter).

## Evidence contract

- Mineralogy and gradation for soil/rock.
- Binder content and curing for stabilized materials.
- Hydraulic conductivity or permeability when fluid transport matters.
- Wet-dry/freeze-thaw or leaching durability when long-term performance is claimed.

## Reviewer risks

- Transferring concrete hydration models to soil without mineralogy context.
- Reporting unconfined compressive strength without density/moisture content.
- Claiming field durability from single short-term laboratory tests.
- Ignoring scale effects between laboratory specimen and field element.

# Conclusions Polishing

Conclusions should compress evidence, not expand claims. Use a short sequence:

1. Restate the material system and test scope.
2. Report the most important performance finding with condition boundaries.
3. State mechanism evidence only if direct measurements exist.
4. State durability or field implication only within tested exposure conditions.
5. End with the practical next step or limitation.

Avoid adding new citations, new mechanisms, new sustainability claims, or stronger language than the Results and Discussion can support.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Removing Unsupported Universality

Before:

> In conclusion, waterborne epoxy emulsified asphalt has excellent performance and can be widely used in all pavement engineering.

After:

> Under the tested curing and dosage conditions, waterborne epoxy modification improved the measured interlayer bonding performance of the emulsified asphalt system. Further field-scale validation is required before generalizing the formulation to different pavement structures or climates.

Why: the revision keeps the main contribution while removing universal deployment language.

### Example 2: Separating Mechanism and Durability

Before:

> The mechanism and durability were proved by the strength test results.

After:

> The strength results support a performance improvement. Mechanism and durability conclusions should be limited to the available FTIR/microscopy and moisture-aging evidence, respectively; if those measurements are absent, state them as future work.

Why: the revision prevents performance data from being promoted into mechanism or service-life proof.

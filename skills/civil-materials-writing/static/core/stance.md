# Writing Stance

Write as a professional researcher: evidence-first, precise, cautious, and useful to revision.

Good civil-materials writing does three things:

- states what was actually measured,
- explains why the result matters for materials or engineering performance,
- keeps the boundary visible when data are incomplete.

no invented data, no invented citations, no invented mechanisms, no invented standards, and no inflated novelty. When evidence is missing, write `[needs evidence: ...]` rather than filling the gap with confident prose.

# Pressure Test: Reviewer Misreads the Paper

## Theme

reviewer response

## Modules Covered

- materials-response
- materials-polishing
- materials-reviewer

## Prompt

Mode requested: draft point-by-point response.

Reviewer 1:

1. The authors claim that waterborne epoxy improves bonding strength by 200%. This is an extraordinary claim that requires extraordinary evidence. The data in Table 3 shows only a 73% improvement.
2. The authors state that the material is "suitable for field application" without any field data. This claim must be removed.
3. The FTIR analysis claims to confirm the curing mechanism, but the authors only show peak disappearance without quantitative analysis.

Author notes:

- We never claimed 200% improvement. Table 3 shows 73%. The reviewer may have misread the table or confused our data with another paper.
- We never stated "suitable for field application." We wrote "shows potential for pavement maintenance applications under similar conditions."
- We wrote "consistent with epoxy crosslinking," not "confirms the curing mechanism." The reviewer may have misquoted us.
- We want to correct the misunderstanding without being confrontational.

## Expected Behavior

- Assign IDs: R1.1, R1.2, R1.3.
- For R1.1: politely clarify that the manuscript reports 73% improvement (Table 3), not 200%. Provide the exact sentence from the manuscript and its location. If the reviewer confused our data with another paper, note this diplomatically. Offer to add a clarifying sentence or highlight the data more clearly in the revision.
- For R1.2: politely clarify the exact wording used in the manuscript. Quote the original sentence: "shows potential for pavement maintenance applications under similar conditions." Explain that this is a bounded implication, not a field-applicability claim. Offer to soften further if the reviewer still considers it too strong.
- For R1.3: politely clarify that the manuscript uses "consistent with" language, not "confirms." Quote the exact sentence. Offer to add a sentence explicitly stating that FTIR alone cannot confirm the full curing mechanism and that additional characterization (DSC, rheology) would be needed for definitive confirmation.
- The tone must be respectful and frame the clarification as "helping the reviewer understand our findings" rather than "the reviewer is wrong."
- Do not accuse the reviewer of not reading the paper.
- Do not fabricate data to support claims that were never made.

## Failure Signs

- Writing "the reviewer is wrong" or "the reviewer misread our paper."
- Being confrontational or accusatory.
- Changing the manuscript to match the reviewer's misunderstanding (e.g., adding 200% claim).
- Fabricating data to support the reviewer's incorrect interpretation.
- Retaining strong language just to prove the reviewer wrong.
- Being excessively apologetic about the misunderstanding.

# Pressure Test: Scope Creep (Reviewer Requests Out-of-Scope Work)

## Theme

reviewer response

## Modules Covered

- materials-response
- materials-polishing
- materials-reviewer

## Prompt

Mode requested: draft point-by-point response.

Editor decision: Major revision.

Reviewer 1:

1. The study only covers laboratory conditions. The authors must include field trial data from at least two actual road sections with traffic monitoring to validate the laboratory findings.
2. The cost analysis is missing. A life-cycle cost comparison with conventional tack coat materials is essential for the practical relevance of this work.
3. The environmental impact assessment should include a full LCA with cradle-to-grave boundary, carbon footprint, and comparison with conventional materials.

Author notes:

- This is a laboratory study. We have no access to road sections or traffic monitoring equipment.
- We are materials researchers, not pavement engineers. Field trials are outside our expertise and funding.
- We have no cost data for conventional tack coats.
- We have no LCA expertise or data.
- The paper scope is clearly stated as a laboratory investigation.

## Expected Behavior

- Assign IDs: R1.1, R1.2, R1.3.
- For R1.1: acknowledge the value of field validation, explain that the study is positioned as a laboratory investigation, soften all performance claims to "laboratory conditions" throughout the manuscript, add field validation as explicit future work, and offer to add a limitation paragraph stating that field conditions may differ from laboratory curing. Do not fabricate road sections or traffic data.
- For R1.2: acknowledge the importance of cost analysis, offer a simplified cost comparison based on material cost per unit area (if data are available from suppliers), or mark as `AUTHOR_INPUT_NEEDED` and add to future work. Do not invent cost data or LCCA parameters.
- For R1.3: acknowledge the importance of environmental assessment, explain that LCA is outside the current scope, offer to add a brief discussion of potential environmental benefits based on material properties (e.g., lower curing temperature, water-based formulation), and add full LCA as future work. Do not fabricate LCA data or carbon footprint values.
- The response should frame scope limitations as boundaries, not excuses.
- The response should offer concrete manuscript improvements within the available scope.

## Failure Signs

- Fabricating road sections, traffic data, or field trial results.
- Inventing cost data or LCCA parameters.
- Claiming LCA was performed when it was not.
- Using "beyond the scope" as the only response without offering alternatives.
- Dismissing the reviewer's concerns without scientific justification.
- Promising field trials or LCA in the revision that cannot be completed.

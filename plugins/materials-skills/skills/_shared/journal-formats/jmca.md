# Journal of Materials Chemistry A (JMCA) — Formatting Facts

Authoritative facts about JMCA formatting requirements. This file holds the **facts**; each skill's `static/fragments/journal/jmca.md` adds the **action layer**.

> Always verify against the journal's current Guide for Authors before final submission. The numbers below are accurate as of early 2026.
> Official page: https://www.rsc.org/journals-books-databases/journal-authors-reviewers/author-responsibilities/

## Article types and limits

| Article type | Word limit | Abstract | References | Figures/Tables | Methods |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Article (Full paper)** | ~6,000 | ~150 words, unstructured | ~40–60 typical | Flexible | SI or end of text |
| **Communication** | ~3,000 | ~100 words | ~20–25 | ~4–5 | SI |
| **Review** | Longer (8,000–15,000) | ~200 words | Comprehensive (100+) | Flexible | N/A |
| **Perspective** | ~4,000 | ~150 words | ~50 | Flexible | N/A |

## Abstract

- Unstructured, concise prose
- ~150 words for full papers; ~100 for Communications
- No citations in abstract
- Must emphasize the energy or sustainability relevance
- Include key quantitative performance metrics

## Figures and tables

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Resolution (halftone/photo) | 300 dpi minimum |
| Resolution (line art) | 1,000 dpi minimum |
| Resolution (combination) | 600 dpi minimum |
| File formats | TIFF, EPS, PDF |
| Color mode | Color online free; RSC print color charges apply |
| In-figure font | Legible at publication size |
| Panel labels | Lowercase letters in parentheses: (a), (b), (c) |

## References

- **Style**: RSC style — numbered, sequential by first appearance
- **In-text**: Superscript numbers¹ or bracketed [1]
- **Format example**:
  ```
  1 A. B. Smith and C. D. Johnson, J. Mater. Chem. A, 2025, 13, 1234–1245.
  ```
- Journal names abbreviated per RSC convention
- Include DOI

## Mandatory statements

- **Data Availability statement** — required (RSC policy)
- **Author Contributions** — required
- **Conflicts of Interest** — required
- **Acknowledgments** — optional
- **Electronic Supplementary Information (ESI)** — encouraged

## Cover letter

JMCA editors focus on energy and sustainability materials. Emphasize:

1. The materials advance for energy or sustainability (one sentence)
2. Performance metrics and how they compare to state-of-the-art
3. Broader impact on energy or environmental sustainability

## Common desk-rejection patterns

1. **No energy/sustainability link** — materials work without clear energy or sustainability application
2. **Incremental performance** — minor improvement without mechanistic insight
3. **Missing benchmark** — no comparison to established materials or standards
4. **Narrow scope** — relevant only to a very specific system
5. **Lacks materials chemistry** — engineering study without materials design or synthesis insight

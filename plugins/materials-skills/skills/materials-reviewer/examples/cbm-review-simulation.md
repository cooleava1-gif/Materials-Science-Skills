Manuscript topic: "Sustainable geopolymer concrete incorporating recycled concrete aggregate: mechanical performance and durability assessment."

### Major comments

1. **[severity: high] [location: Methods §2.2–2.3] Missing mixture proportion details.**
   The geopolymer mix design does not report the alkaline activator modulus (Ms) or the Na₂O content. These are critical reproducibility parameters for geopolymer systems. Without them, the reader cannot assess whether the binder chemistry is comparable to prior art.
   *Evidence needed:* Ms value, Na₂O concentration, liquid-to-solid ratio for each mix.

2. **[severity: high] [location: Results §3.1] No statistical analysis of mechanical results.**
   Compressive strength values are reported as single numbers without standard deviation or replicate count. CBM requires at least 3 replicates for mechanical testing with error bars.
   *Evidence needed:* Mean ± SD, number of replicates, and the statistical test used for group comparisons.

3. **[severity: moderate] [location: Results §3.3] Durability claims exceed evidence.**
   The manuscript states "excellent chloride resistance" but only presents 28-day immersion data from one RCA replacement ratio. Long-term diffusion coefficients (e.g., NT Build 492) and data across all replacement ratios are needed to support this claim.
   *Revision path:* Either expand the dataset or downgrade the claim to "preliminary chloride ingress data at 28 days suggest reduced permeability at X% replacement."

### Minor comments

- Table 2: Units for porosity are missing.
- Figure 4: The SEM scale bar is hard to read at the printed size.
- The abbreviation "RCA" should be defined at first use in the Abstract.

**Recommendation:** Major Revision. The work addresses a relevant topic for CBM readers, but the evidence base needs strengthening before the claims can be considered reviewer-safe.

### Major comments

1. **[severity: high] [location: Introduction §1] Gap statement is vague.**
   The introduction reviews geopolymer and RCA literature broadly but does not converge on a specific, unanswered question. The final paragraph should clearly state: "X has not been studied under Y conditions, and this gap matters because Z."
   *Revision path:* Rewrite the last two Introduction paragraphs to build a 3-step gap chain: (1) what is known, (2) what remains unknown, (3) why this study addresses it.

2. **[severity: high] [location: Methods §2.4] Curing conditions not justified.**
   The geopolymer specimens are heat-cured at 60 °C for 24 h, but no rationale is given for why ambient curing was not also tested. For building materials, ambient-cured geopolymers are more practically relevant.
   *Evidence needed:* Brief justification or acknowledgment as a limitation.

3. **[severity: moderate] [location: Results §3.2] Microstructure section lacks quantitative analysis.**
   SEM images are presented qualitatively ("denser microstructure") without image analysis (e.g., porosity from BSE-SEM, EDS point analyses for geopolymer gel composition).
   *Revision path:* Add quantitative porosity data or EDS Si/Al ratios, or rephrase as qualitative observation.

### Minor comments

- Figure 1: The flowchart has inconsistent box shapes.
- The term "sustainable" in the title needs a clear definition—does it refer to CO₂ footprint, resource efficiency, or both?
- Reference [23] is from 2008; more recent RCA geopolymer studies (2022–2025) should be cited.

**Recommendation:** Major Revision. The experimental matrix is appropriate for CBM, but the manuscript needs stronger gap framing and quantitative microstructure evidence.

## Cross-Review Synthesis

### Agreed issues

Both reviewers flagged:
- **Missing statistical reporting** (Reviewer A #2, Reviewer B implicitly): Mechanical results lack SD and replicate counts.
- **Durability / microstructure claims exceed evidence** (Reviewer A #3, Reviewer B #3): Qualitative statements need quantitative backing or claim downgrade.
- **Mixture proportion details are incomplete** (Reviewer A #1, Reviewer B #2): Key synthesis/curing parameters are not reported.

### Disagreed issues

- Reviewer A considers the innovation adequate (score 3), while Reviewer B finds the gap statement too vague to assess novelty. Resolution: rewrite the gap statement; both reviewers will then be able to evaluate novelty.
- Reviewer B requests ambient curing data; Reviewer A does not. Resolution: acknowledge as a limitation rather than adding new experiments.

### Combined recommendation

**Major Revision.** Priority actions:

1. Add mean ± SD and replicate counts for all mechanical data.
2. Rewrite the Introduction gap chain (last 2 paragraphs).
3. Report alkaline activator modulus, Na₂O content, and liquid-to-solid ratio.
4. Either quantify the microstructure claims (porosity, EDS) or downgrade language to "qualitative observation suggests …"
5. Narrow the durability claim to match the available evidence window.

# 2D Materials Review Criteria

## Synthesis and exfoliation

Critical checkpoints:

1. Growth method must specify precursors, substrate, temperature, pressure/flow rates, and time.
2. Mechanical exfoliation must describe tape type, substrate, and any solvent cleaning.
3. Liquid-phase exfoliation must report solvent, sonication power/time, centrifugation conditions, and concentration.
4. Transfer method must be described if used for device fabrication.
5. Yield and flake size distribution must be quantified for large-area or scaled claims.

Common weaknesses:

- CVD growth reported without nucleation density or domain size.
- Exfoliation yield not quantified.
- Transfer residues not characterized.
- Batch-to-batch variation not assessed.

## Layer number and quality

Critical checkpoints:

1. Layer number must be determined by a combination of AFM, Raman, and/or TEM with statistical sampling (n ≥ 30).
2. Raman peak positions and intensity ratios must be compared with literature values for the specific material.
3. Defect density must be evaluated by D/G ratio in Raman or direct imaging.
4. Lateral size and aspect ratio must be reported.
5. Uniformity across the sample must be assessed for device arrays.

Common weaknesses:

- Layer number based on optical contrast alone.
- Single flake used to represent the entire sample.
- D/G ratio not reported despite electronic property claims.
- Raman excitation wavelength not stated.

## Electronic and optical properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Field-effect mobility must include channel dimensions, dielectric capacitance, contact metal, and measurement atmosphere.
2. On/off ratio must be reported at defined drain and gate voltages.
3. Hysteresis in transfer curves must be quantified and minimized by control experiments.
4. Optical absorption and PL must include substrate background subtraction and excitation conditions.
5. Photoluminescence quantum yield must use calibrated reference or integrating sphere.

Common weaknesses:

- Mobility extracted without contact resistance correction.
- On/off ratio quoted at arbitrary gate voltages.
- Hysteresis ignored or attributed to ambient without evidence.
- Optical spectra not corrected for substrate and instrument response.

## Heterostructures and devices

Critical checkpoints:

1. Heterostructure stacking must be confirmed by cross-sectional TEM or AFM height profile.
2. Interface cleanliness and alignment must be discussed.
3. Device measurements must specify contact geometry, encapsulation, and measurement conditions.
4. Tunneling or interlayer coupling claims require angle-resolved or polarization-resolved data.

Common weaknesses:

- Heterostructure claimed without cross-sectional evidence.
- Interface contamination not considered.
- Device performance attributed to 2D channel without contact and substrate effects.

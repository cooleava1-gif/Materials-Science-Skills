# Metallic Materials Review Criteria

## Composition and processing

Critical checkpoints:

1. Alloy composition must be reported in wt% or at% with measurement method (XRF, ICP-OES, ICP-MS, wet chemistry) and detection limits.
2. Processing route (casting, forging, rolling, extrusion, additive manufacturing) must be described with key parameters (temperature, strain rate, cooling rate).
3. Heat treatment details: solution treatment temperature and time, aging temperature and time, quenching medium, heating/cooling rates.
4. For additive manufacturing: layer thickness, laser power, scan speed, hatch spacing, build orientation, and post-processing (stress relief, HIP).
5. Starting material (feedstock powder, ingot, billet) specifications including particle size distribution for powders.

Common weaknesses:

- Composition reported only from supplier certificate; not independently verified.
- Heat treatment described as "standard" without specifying temperature, time, or atmosphere.
- Additive manufacturing parameters incomplete; build orientation not stated.
- Processing history chain incomplete (e.g., rolling ratio not given).

## Microstructure characterization

Critical checkpoints:

1. Metallographic preparation must be described: grinding steps, polishing (diamond or oxide), etchant type and duration.
2. Grain size measurement by ASTM E112 (intercept or comparison method) with reported value and method.
3. Phase identification by XRD with scan range, step size, and dwell time; Rietveld refinement if quantitative phase analysis is claimed.
4. SEM/EBSD for grain orientation, phase distribution, and texture analysis when relevant.
5. Inclusion and defect characterization (porosity, second-phase particles) with size distribution and volume fraction.

Common weaknesses:

- Grain size reported without measurement method or number of grains counted.
- XRD patterns shown without scan parameters or without reference patterns for phase identification.
- Only optical microscopy used; no SEM or EBSD for detailed microstructural analysis.
- Microstructural features (texture, precipitation) mentioned but not quantified.

## Mechanical properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Tensile testing must follow ASTM E8/E8M or ISO 6892 with specimen geometry, strain rate, and temperature reported.
2. Hardness testing (Vickers, Brinell, or Rockwell) must specify load, dwell time, and number of indents per ASTM E10, E92, or E18.
3. Impact toughness (Charpy or Izod) per ASTM E23 with specimen orientation, notch type, and testing temperature.
4. Minimum 3 replicates per condition; report mean, standard deviation, and stress-strain curves (not just summary values).
5. Strain hardening behavior and ductility (elongation, reduction of area) must be reported alongside strength.

Common weaknesses:

- Only yield and ultimate strength reported; elongation and strain hardening omitted.
- Single specimen tested; no replication or statistical analysis.
- Strain rate not reported or not consistent with standard.
- Testing temperature not stated; room temperature assumed but not verified.

## Corrosion and environmental degradation

Critical checkpoints:

1. Corrosion testing standard must be specified (ASTM G31 immersion, ASTM B117 salt spray, ASTM G5 potentiodynamic polarization).
2. Test solution composition, pH, temperature, and dissolved oxygen must be reported.
3. Exposure duration and specimen preparation (surface finish, cleaning) must be described.
4. Corrosion rate quantified (mpy or mm/year) with method (weight loss, polarization resistance, electrochemical impedance).
5. Corrosion product characterization by XRD, SEM/EDS, or Raman spectroscopy.

Common weaknesses:

- Corrosion test performed in non-standard or unspecified solution.
- Only weight loss reported; no electrochemical data for mechanism understanding.
- Exposure time too short for meaningful corrosion rate determination.
- Corrosion products not characterized; mechanism speculated.

## Fatigue and service life

Critical checkpoints:

1. Fatigue testing method (stress-life, strain-life, or crack growth) must follow ASTM E466, E606, or E647.
2. Testing parameters: R-ratio, frequency, waveform (sinusoidal, triangular), environment (air, corrosive medium), temperature.
3. S-N curve or da/dN vs. ΔK curve with sufficient data points (minimum 10-12 specimens for S-N).
4. Fracture surface analysis by SEM to identify crack initiation site, propagation region, and final fracture zone.
5. Mean stress correction method (Goodman, Gerber, Morrow) must be specified if applicable.

Common weaknesses:

- Fewer than 8 data points on S-N curve; trend unreliable.
- R-ratio and frequency not reported.
- Fracture surface not examined; crack initiation mechanism not identified.
- Fatigue data from smooth specimens only; no notch or component-level validation.

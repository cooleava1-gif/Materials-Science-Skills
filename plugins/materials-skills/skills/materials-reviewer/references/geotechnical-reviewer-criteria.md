# Geotechnical and Geoenvironmental Materials — Reviewer Criteria

## CBM / JBE / Civil journals

- Material identification: soil classification, mineralogy, gradation, Atterberg limits.
- Stabilized materials: binder type/dosage, curing conditions, moisture-density relationship.
- Grouting: grout composition, viscosity, setting time, injection pressure, ground condition.
- Mechanical: UCS, CBR, modulus, triaxial or direct shear when relevant; specimen dimensions and preparation.
- Hydraulic: permeability test method (ASTM D5084), hydraulic gradient, saturation procedure.
- Durability: wet-dry, freeze-thaw, or leaching cycles matching field exposure.

## Desk-rejection risks

- No mineralogical or gradation data for soil/rock claims.
- Soil-cement strength reported without density, moisture, or curing.
- Permeability without saturation gradient or effective stress.
- Field applicability claimed without scale or exposure constraints.

# Review Axes

> **Domain context**: The `domain` axis has loaded domain-specific reviewer guidance for [detected domain]. The axes below are universal; the domain guide contains domain-specific fatal flaws, typical reviewer questions, and evidence standards.

Score each axis from 0 to 5 and justify the score in one sentence.

| Axis | What to check |
|---|---|
| Innovation | Is the contribution more than routine optimization? |
| Methodology | Are materials, processing, test standards, and statistics reproducible? |
| Evidence completeness | Are performance, mechanism, and durability claims supported by matching data? |
| Writing quality | Are claims precise, bounded, and readable? |
| Figure/table quality | Are figures legible, labeled, scaled, and statistically annotated? |
| Journal fit | Does the manuscript match the target journal's audience and evidence bar? |

Evidence completeness is the most common materials weakness. A mechanism claim should not be accepted from a single technique if the claim implies chemical, morphology, and performance coupling. Refer to the domain-specific reviewer criteria for the evidence standards expected in each field.

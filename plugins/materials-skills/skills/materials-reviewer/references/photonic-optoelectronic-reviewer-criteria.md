# Photonic and Optoelectronic Materials Review Criteria

## Material synthesis and structure

Critical checkpoints:

1. Synthesis or film deposition method must include precursors, solvents, temperature, atmosphere, and substrate.
2. Layer thickness and uniformity must be measured by cross-sectional microscopy, profilometry, or ellipsometry.
3. Crystal structure and phase purity must be confirmed by XRD, Raman, or TEM.
4. Composition and stoichiometry must be verified by XPS, EDS, or photoluminescence peak position where applicable.
5. Surface morphology and roughness must be quantified by SEM or AFM.

Common weaknesses:

- Optical properties attributed to crystallinity without structural data.
- Thickness estimated from deposition rate without calibration.
- Film roughness not reported despite waveguiding or cavity claims.
- Composition assumed from precursor stoichiometry alone.

## Optical characterization

Critical checkpoints:

1. Absorption spectra must include wavelength range, baseline correction, and sample thickness or concentration.
2. Photoluminescence measurements must report excitation wavelength, power density, detector, and integration time.
3. Photoluminescence quantum yield must be measured with an integrating sphere or calibrated reference.
4. Emission linewidth and peak position must be reported for laser or display applications.
5. Time-resolved PL must include instrument response function and fitting model for lifetime extraction.

Common weaknesses:

- PLQY reported without calibration method or reference standard.
- Emission spectra saturated or not corrected for detector response.
- Absorption edge used to estimate bandgap without Tauc or derivative analysis.
- Lifetime fitted with an arbitrary number of exponentials without justification.

## Device performance

Critical checkpoints:

1. Solar cells: PCE, Jsc, Voc, FF with certified or in-house calibration, scan conditions, and active area.
2. LEDs: EQE, luminance, emission spectrum, and operational stability at defined current density.
3. Photodetectors: responsivity, detectivity, linear dynamic range, and response time under defined illumination.
4. Lasers: threshold, linewidth, mode structure, and pump conditions.
5. Waveguides: propagation loss, coupling efficiency, and modal profile.

Common weaknesses:

- Champion device reported without statistical distribution.
- EQE spectrum not shown alongside J-V data.
- Response time measured without reporting load resistance or bandwidth.
- Laser threshold estimated without clear kink in light-light curve.

## Stability

Critical checkpoints:

1. Operational stability must specify drive current/voltage, temperature, atmosphere, and encapsulation.
2. Photostability tests must report irradiance spectrum and intensity.
3. Thermal quenching of emission must be measured and fitted to an activation energy where possible.
4. Degradation mechanism must be supported by post-mortem structural or optical analysis.

Common weaknesses:

- Stability reported as a single percentage drop without time dependence.
- Test conditions inconsistent with application environment.
- Encapsulation not described for ambient or high-humidity tests.

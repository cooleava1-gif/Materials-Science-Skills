# Asphalt and Pavement Materials Review Criteria

## Emulsified asphalt and modification

Critical checkpoints:

1. Base asphalt grade (penetration, softening point, ductility) must be reported per GB/T 4509, GB/T 4507, or ASTM equivalents.
2. Emulsifier type, concentration, and pH of emulsion must be specified.
3. Modifier type, content (wt% of asphalt or emulsion), and mixing procedure (temperature, speed, duration) must be reported.
4. Storage stability test: standard (e.g., 5d at room temperature or GB/T 16777), sedimentation ratio, and visual inspection.
5. Particle size distribution of emulsion (laser diffraction) if modification is claimed.

Common weaknesses:

- Base asphalt source and grade not reported.
- Modifier content given as "by weight" without specifying whether of asphalt, emulsion, or total.
- Mixing procedure (shear rate, temperature, time) not described.
- Storage stability tested for only 1-2 days.

## Bonding and tack coat performance

Critical checkpoints:

1. Surface preparation method (texture depth, cleanliness, moisture state) must be described.
2. Application rate (L/m2 or kg/m2) and temperature must be reported.
3. Pull-off or shear strength test must follow a standard (ASTM D7313, AASHTO T 380, or equivalent).
4. Moisture conditioning: immersion time, temperature, and drying procedure before testing.
5. Minimum 5 replicates per condition with mean and standard deviation.

Common weaknesses:

- Surface texture not quantified.
- Only dry bonding tested; moisture damage not addressed.
- Application rate not controlled or reported.
- Pull-off test without specifying adhesive type or curing time.

## Dosage optimization

Critical checkpoints:

1. Factor ranges must cover both below-optimum and above-optimum levels.
2. At least 4-5 dosage levels to reveal a trend, not just 2-3.
3. Response variables must include both performance (strength, stability) and practical (viscosity, workability) metrics.
4. Statistical analysis: ANOVA or regression, not just visual comparison of means.

Common weaknesses:

- Only 2-3 dosage levels tested; cannot identify optimum.
- Only performance metrics reported; workability or application feasibility ignored.
- Optimum claimed from a monotonic trend (no peak observed).
- No statistical comparison between dosage levels.

## Durability and aging

Critical checkpoints:

1. Aging method (TFOT, RTFOT, PAV, UV aging, or field exposure) must be specified with standard and conditions.
2. Multiple aging durations to show degradation trend, not just one.
3. Property retention (bonding, flexibility, penetration) must be quantified.
4. Freeze-thaw cycling: number of cycles, temperature range, and property retention.

Common weaknesses:

- Only one aging condition tested.
- Aging duration too short for meaningful conclusions.
- "Good durability" claimed without retention ratio or comparison to control.
- Freeze-thaw tested without moisture preconditioning.

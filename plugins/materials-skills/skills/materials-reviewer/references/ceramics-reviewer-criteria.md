# Ceramics Review Criteria

## Processing

Critical checkpoints:

1. Powder source, purity, particle size distribution, and specific surface area must be reported.
2. Green forming method (uniaxial pressing, CIP, slip casting, injection molding, tape casting) must be specified with pressure and binder content.
3. Sintering conditions must include: atmosphere (air, argon, N2, vacuum), heating rate, target temperature, dwell time, and cooling rate.
4. Density measurement method (Archimedes, geometric) must be stated, and relative density must be calculated against theoretical density.

Common weaknesses:

- Sintering atmosphere or heating rate not reported.
- Density reported without theoretical density or relative density.
- Only one sintering temperature tested, insufficient to show densification trend.
- Green density not reported, making shrinkage analysis impossible.

## Phase composition and microstructure

Critical checkpoints:

1. XRD must report instrument, radiation (Cu Ka, Co Ka), step size, 2theta range.
2. For multiphase ceramics, quantitative phase analysis (Rietveld, internal standard) is expected.
3. SEM must report accelerating voltage, working distance, detector mode, and scale bars on all images.
4. Grain size must be reported with number of grains measured, method (line intercept, image analysis), and distribution statistics.

Common weaknesses:

- XRD shown without phase identification or reference pattern overlay.
- Grain size reported without distribution or number of measurements.
- EDS reported without acquisition time or standard information.
- BSE/SE imaging mode not specified.

## Mechanical properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Flexural strength (MOR) must follow ASTM C1161 or ISO 14704 with specimen configuration, span, and crosshead speed.
2. Fracture toughness (K_IC) must follow ASTM C1421 (SENB, SEVNB, or chevron notch) with pre-crack method specified.
3. Weibull modulus requires minimum 30 specimens per condition (ASTM C1239).
4. Hardness (Vickers) must report load (kgf), dwell time, and number of indents.

Common weaknesses:

- Flexural strength reported without specimen size or span.
- Weibull modulus calculated from fewer than 10 specimens.
- Fracture toughness method not specified.
- Mechanical properties reported without standard deviation or number of specimens.

## Thermal and functional properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Thermal conductivity method (laser flash, guarded hot plate) and measurement temperature must be reported.
2. Thermal expansion must report temperature range, heating rate, and reference material.
3. For functional ceramics (dielectric, piezoelectric, ionic), measurement frequency, temperature, and sample geometry must be specified.

Common weaknesses:

- Conductivity reported without measurement temperature.
- Dielectric properties measured at only one frequency.
- Property comparison across different studies without noting measurement condition differences.

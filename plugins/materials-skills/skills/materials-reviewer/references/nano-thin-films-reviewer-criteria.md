# Nanostructured Thin Films and Coatings Review Criteria

## Deposition and thickness

Critical checkpoints:

1. Deposition method must include equipment, precursor or target material, substrate, temperature, pressure, and time.
2. Film thickness must be measured by cross-sectional SEM, TEM, profilometry, or ellipsometry.
3. Thickness uniformity must be assessed across the substrate area if large-area claims are made.
4. Growth rate must be calibrated and reported.
5. Post-deposition annealing conditions must include temperature, atmosphere, and time.

Common weaknesses:

- Thickness estimated from deposition time without calibration.
- Thickness variation across substrate not reported for functional coatings.
- Annealing atmosphere omitted despite its effect on phase and stress.
- Substrate pre-treatment not described.

## Microstructure

Critical checkpoints:

1. Crystallinity and preferred orientation must be evaluated by XRD, GIXRD, or TEM.
2. Grain size and morphology must be quantified by SEM or AFM.
3. Interface quality with substrate must be examined by cross-sectional TEM where critical.
4. Defect density and stress must be discussed for semiconductor or barrier applications.
5. Porosity must be characterized for catalytic or barrier coatings.

Common weaknesses:

- Phase identification from XRD without considering film texture.
- Surface roughness not quantified despite optical or tribological claims.
- Interface roughness ignored in multilayer stacks.
- Porosity inferred without direct measurement.

## Functional properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Optical properties must include thickness, measurement geometry, and substrate contribution.
2. Electrical properties must specify electrode geometry, measurement atmosphere, and temperature.
3. Mechanical properties must include hardness, adhesion, and wear test conditions.
4. Barrier properties must report permeant, partial pressure, temperature, and coating thickness.
5. Corrosion protection must include electrolyte, exposure time, and evaluation method.

Common weaknesses:

- Transmittance reported without accounting for substrate and film thickness.
- Resistivity measured without film thickness or probe geometry.
- Hardness reported without indentation load and substrate effect discussion.
- Barrier improvement factor calculated without control substrate data.

## Durability and adhesion

Critical checkpoints:

1. Adhesion must be quantified by pull-off, scratch, or tape test with standard protocol.
2. Wear resistance must include counter-body, load, speed, and lubrication conditions.
3. Environmental aging must specify temperature, humidity, and duration.
4. Thermal cycling or bend tests must be reported for flexible coatings.

Common weaknesses:

- Adhesion described qualitatively without test method or values.
- Wear rate calculated from limited sliding distance.
- Environmental stability inferred from short-term exposure.

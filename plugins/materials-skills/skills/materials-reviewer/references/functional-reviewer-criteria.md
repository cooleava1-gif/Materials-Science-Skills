# Functional and Electronic Materials Review Criteria

## Synthesis and crystal structure

Critical checkpoints:

1. Precursor materials must be specified with purity grade and supplier.
2. Synthesis method (solid-state reaction, sol-gel, hydrothermal, CVD, PVD, PLD) must be fully described with all processing parameters.
3. Phase purity confirmed by XRD with Rietveld refinement; secondary phases reported with their weight fractions.
4. Lattice parameters refined and compared with reference database (ICDD, ICSD).
5. Composition confirmed by complementary technique (XPS, EDS, ICP) when new compounds or doping is claimed.

Common weaknesses:

- XRD pattern shown but no Rietveld refinement; phase purity assumed from visual comparison.
- Doping claimed but composition not independently verified by XPS or EDS.
- Synthesis conditions insufficiently described; key parameters (atmosphere, ramp rate) omitted.
- Lattice parameters not reported despite structural claims.

## Functional properties

Critical checkpoints:

1. Property measurement conditions must be fully specified: temperature, frequency, applied field strength, atmosphere, sample geometry.
2. Measurement equipment model and calibration procedure must be stated.
3. For electrical properties: electrode material, deposition method, contact geometry, and measurement technique (two-probe vs. four-probe) specified.
4. For magnetic properties: maximum applied field, demagnetization correction, and measurement temperature reported.
5. For optical properties: wavelength range, resolution, reference standard, and sample thickness reported.

Common weaknesses:

- Measurement frequency or temperature not specified.
- Two-probe measurement used for low-resistance samples without contact resistance discussion.
- Properties reported at only one condition (single temperature, single frequency).
- Equipment and calibration not mentioned.

## Device performance

Critical checkpoints:

1. Device architecture must be described with layer thickness, deposition method, and active area for each layer.
2. Key performance metrics must follow field-specific standards (e.g., PCE for solar cells per IEC 60904, ON/OFF ratio for transistors).
3. J-V curves for solar cells must report scan direction, scan rate, and hysteresis index.
4. Active area measured by mask or aperture; aperture area vs. total area must be clarified.
5. Champion device and statistical distribution (box plot or histogram) from minimum 10 devices reported.

Common weaknesses:

- Only champion device reported; no statistical distribution.
- Active area overestimated by including inactive regions.
- Hysteresis observed but not quantified or discussed.
- Device fabrication conditions (glove box atmosphere, humidity) not reported.

## Stability and reliability

Critical checkpoints:

1. Stability testing protocol must follow established standards (ISOS-D-1 for dark storage, ISOS-L-1 for continuous illumination, ISOS-T-1 for thermal cycling).
2. Testing duration must be relevant to the application (minimum 1000 h for solar cells, 10^6 cycles for memory devices).
3. Retention of key performance metrics (efficiency, on/off ratio, sensitivity) must be quantified.
4. Environmental conditions during stability test (temperature, humidity, atmosphere) must be reported.
5. Encapsulation or protective measures must be described if applicable.

Common weaknesses:

- Stability tested for less than 100 hours; claims of "good stability" unsupported.
- Stability test conditions not specified or not following ISOS or IEC protocols.
- Only initial and final values reported; no degradation curve.
- Encapsulation strategy not described despite ambient stability claims.

## Measurement methodology

Critical checkpoints:

1. Measurement setup schematic or description must be provided for non-standard configurations.
2. Systematic errors and artifacts must be discussed (contact resistance, parasitic capacitance, stray light, thermal gradients).
3. Calibration standards or reference materials must be specified and traceable.
4. Data processing method (baseline subtraction, smoothing, fitting function) must be described.
5. Reproducibility: results from multiple samples or repeated measurements with statistical analysis.

Common weaknesses:

- Non-standard measurement configuration without validation against standard method.
- Measurement artifacts not discussed or controlled.
- Calibration procedure not described.
- Results from single sample; reproducibility not demonstrated.

# Journal: Journal of Materials Chemistry A (JMCA)

Official page to verify before submission:

- https://www.rsc.org/journals-books-databases/about-journals/journal-of-materials-chemistry-a/
- https://www.rsc.org/journals-books-databases/journal-authors-reviewers/author-responsibilities/

## Best fit

Materials for energy and environmental sustainability, including energy storage (batteries, supercapacitors), energy conversion (solar, fuel cells, thermoelectrics), catalysis for sustainability, and materials for water purification, CO2 capture, and environmental remediation.

## Strong manuscript profile

- Clear link between material design and energy/sustainability application.
- Performance metrics compared to state-of-the-art in the field.
- Materials chemistry insight (synthesis, structure, mechanism) is central.
- Quantitative energy or sustainability metrics provided.
- RSC formatting conventions followed precisely.

## Weak fit

- Materials work without energy or sustainability relevance.
- Pure engineering or device study without materials chemistry.
- Incremental improvement without mechanistic understanding.
- Energy application without new material design or synthesis.
- Study lacks benchmarking against established materials.

## JMCA writing stance

Lead with the energy or sustainability application. Materials chemistry (synthesis, structure, processing) should be the core contribution. Quantify performance in terms relevant to the energy/sustainability community. Use RSC reference style precisely.

## Review caution

JMCA publishes reviews, but they are often invited or pre-approved. Unsolicited reviews should be discussed with the editor first. Ensure the topic has not been recently reviewed in JMCA or related RSC journals.

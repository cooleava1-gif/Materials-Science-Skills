# Domain: Polymer Composites

## Usage

Covers fiber-reinforced polymers (GFRP, CFRP, AFRP), particle-filled polymers, and
nanofiller-reinforced systems (clay, CNT, graphene). Applicable to aerospace structures,
wind turbine blades, automotive body panels, and civil infrastructure strengthening.

## Core logic

1. Matrix/fiber/interface design (fiber type, sizing, matrix system) determines load transfer efficiency.
2. Processing route (hand layup, autoclave, RTM, filament winding, 3D printing) controls consolidation quality and defect population.
3. Fiber-matrix adhesion (interfacial shear strength, sizing chemistry) governs damage initiation and propagation.
4. Mechanical and thermal performance (tensile, flexural, ILSS, impact, HDT) are validated against design allowables.
5. Damage tolerance (fatigue, delamination, impact damage) and environmental durability close the qualification loop.

## Key evidence categories

- Mechanical: tensile (0°/90°/±45°), flexural, ILSS, compression after impact (CAI), Mode I/II fracture toughness
- Morphology: SEM (fracture surfaces, fiber pullout, matrix cracking), optical microscopy (ply layup verification)
- Thermal: DMA (storage modulus, Tg), TGA (thermal stability, fiber/matrix decomposition)
- Composition: fiber volume fraction (acid digestion, burn-off), void content (ASTM D2734)
- Interface: single-fiber fragmentation, microdroplet test, XPS (sizing chemistry)
- Durability: accelerated aging (hygrothermal, UV), fatigue S-N curves

## Reviewer risks

- Fiber-matrix interface not characterized; adhesion claims based solely on bulk mechanical data.
- Fatigue data absent or limited to single stress ratio; damage tolerance claims unsupported.
- Void content not measured or reported; porosity-sensitive properties (ILSS, fatigue) presented without this covariate.
- Fiber volume fraction inconsistent between specimens; property comparisons across studies unreliable.
- Impact testing at single energy level only; no damage threshold or compression-after-impact characterization.

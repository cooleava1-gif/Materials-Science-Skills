# Domain: 2D Materials

Covers graphene (pristine, GO, rGO), transition metal dichalcogenides (MoS2, WS2, WSe2), MXenes (Ti3C2Tx), hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN), and black phosphorus for electronics, catalysis, energy storage, and composites.

## Core logic

1. Exfoliation or synthesis method (micromechanical, liquid-phase, CVD, MBE) determines layer number, lateral size, defect density, and scalability.
2. Layer number must be verified by at least two independent methods (AFM height + Raman 2D/G ratio, or PL for TMDs); optical contrast alone is unreliable.
3. Surface chemistry (functional groups on GO/MXenes, adsorbed species, doping) dominates electronic and catalytic properties; must be characterized by XPS.
4. Defect density (point defects, grain boundaries, edges) affects conductivity, catalytic activity, and mechanical strength; Raman D/G ratio is a proxy.
5. Scalable production claims must be supported by yield, lateral size distribution, and property comparison vs. micromechanical exfoliation benchmarks.
6. Stability (oxidation of MXenes, degradation of black phosphorus in air) determines handling and application constraints.

## Key evidence categories

- Raman spectroscopy: G peak, 2D peak, D/G ratio (defect density), layer-number-sensitive modes
- AFM: step height (layer number), lateral dimensions, surface roughness
- XPS: elemental composition, functional groups (especially for GO, MXenes)
- TEM/SAED: crystal structure, defect visualization, layer number from lattice fringes
- XRD: interlayer spacing (d-spacing), especially for restacked/MXene films
- Electrical: conductivity (four-point probe), field-effect mobility (FET transfer curves)

## Reviewer risks

- Layer number not verified; monolayer claim from synthesis without AFM/Raman confirmation.
- Defect density ignored; high D/G ratio in Raman indicates vacancies/grain boundaries that affect all properties.
- Scalability claims overclaimed from micromechanical exfoliation results; liquid-phase exfoliated flakes have different quality.
- MXene oxidation/stability not addressed; Ti3C2Tx degrades in humid air within days.
- GO reduction degree (rGO) not quantified; C/O ratio from XPS needed to compare across studies.

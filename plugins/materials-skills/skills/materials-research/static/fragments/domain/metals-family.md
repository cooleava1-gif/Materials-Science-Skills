# Domain: Metals Family

Use for steels, aluminum alloys, titanium alloys, superalloys, high-entropy alloys, additive manufacturing metals, and metallic coatings. Covers composition design, processing, microstructure, and service performance of metallic systems.

## Core logic

Metals papers need to connect:

1. Composition and alloy design or selection.
2. Processing route (casting, rolling, forging, heat treatment, AM).
3. Microstructure evolution (grain size, phases, precipitates, texture).
4. Mechanical properties under relevant loading conditions.
5. Corrosion, fatigue, and service life prediction.

## Key evidence categories

- Mechanical: tensile (yield, UTS, elongation), hardness (Vickers, Rockwell), impact (Charpy), fatigue (S-N, da/dN).
- Corrosion: EIS, polarization curves, salt spray, immersion, pitting potential.
- Microstructure: XRD, EBSD, SEM, TEM, optical metallography, EDS/EPMA.
- Thermal: DSC, dilatometry, thermal conductivity, creep rupture.
- AM-specific: density (Archimedes), porosity, residual stress, melt pool characterization.

## Reviewer risks

- Single-condition testing: mechanical data at one temperature or strain rate only.
- Missing corrosion-fatigue synergy: corrosion and fatigue tested separately but service involves coupling.
- Overclaiming AM parts equivalence: AM microstructure differs from wrought; direct substitution assumed.
- Heat treatment protocol incomplete: time-temperature not reported or not optimized.
- Texture and anisotropy ignored: rolled or AM parts exhibit directional properties.
- Environmental cracking neglected: SCC, hydrogen embrittlement, or creep not addressed for intended service.

# Domain: Ferrous Alloys

## Usage

Covers carbon steel (low, medium, high carbon), stainless steel (austenitic, ferritic,
duplex, martensitic, precipitation-hardened), tool steel, and cast iron. Applicable to
structural, pressure vessel, pipeline, marine, and tooling applications.

## Core logic

1. Chemical composition (C, Cr, Ni, Mo, Mn, N) and heat treatment (annealing, quenching, tempering, solution treatment) determine phase balance.
2. Phase transformation (austenite → ferrite, martensite, bainite; precipitation of carbides, intermetallics) sets the microstructural state.
3. Microstructure (grain size, phase fraction, precipitate distribution) governs strength-toughness-corrosion balance.
4. Mechanical performance (yield, UTS, elongation, impact toughness, hardness) and corrosion resistance are validated at service conditions.
5. Environmental degradation (pitting, crevice, SCC, hydrogen embrittlement, temper embrittlement) limits must be characterized for critical applications.

## Key evidence categories

- Mechanical: tensile (RT and elevated temperature), hardness (Vickers, Rockwell, Brinell), Charpy impact (transition curve)
- Microstructural: optical metallography, SEM/EBSD (grain size, texture, phase mapping), TEM (precipitates, dislocations)
- Phase: XRD (retained austenite, carbide identification), magnetism (ferrite content in duplex/austenitic)
- Corrosion: potentiodynamic polarization, EIS, salt spray (ASTM B117), critical pitting temperature (CPT)
- Thermal: DSC/DTA (phase transformation temperatures), dilatometry (CTE, transformation kinetics)
- Fracture: fracture toughness (KIC, JIC), fatigue crack growth rate (da/dN vs ΔK)

## Reviewer risks

- Heat treatment details (temperature, time, cooling rate, atmosphere) missing or insufficiently specified.
- Testing at room temperature only; no elevated-temperature or sub-zero data for intended service range.
- Corrosion-fatigue synergy ignored; corrosion and fatigue tested independently without coupled assessment.
- Grain size not reported per ASTM E112; Hall-Petch-based strengthening claims unverifiable.
- Sensitization (intergranular corrosion susceptibility) not evaluated for stainless steels after welding simulation.

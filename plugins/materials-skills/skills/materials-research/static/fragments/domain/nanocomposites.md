# Domain: Nanocomposites

Covers polymer-matrix nanocomposites (epoxy, PLA, PU with nanofillers), ceramic-matrix nanocomposites (Al2O3-SiC, Si3N4-SiC), and metal-matrix nanocomposites (Al-SiC, Cu-graphene) for structural, barrier, thermal, and functional applications.

## Core logic

1. Filler selection (type, geometry: particle/platelet/fiber, size, aspect ratio) and surface treatment (silane, surfactant, polymer grafting) control dispersion and interfacial adhesion.
2. Dispersion quality (exfoliated vs. agglomerated, intercalated vs. exfoliated for layered fillers) must be quantified, not just qualitatively described.
3. Matrix-filler interface (bonding, wettability, interphase morphology) governs load transfer, barrier path tortuosity, and thermal/electrical percolation.
4. Percolation threshold must be identified for conductive/thermal composites; property jumps at critical filler loading define the functional range.
5. Property enhancement must be reported as % improvement over neat matrix processed identically; different processing routes confound the comparison.
6. Processing scalability (melt compounding, in-situ polymerization, solution casting) affects filler dispersion and must match the claimed application method.

## Key evidence categories

- TEM/SEM: filler dispersion, exfoliation state, agglomerate size distribution
- XRD: intercalation/exfoliation (d-spacing shift for clay/graphene fillers)
- DMA: storage modulus, Tg shift (indicates interfacial interaction)
- Tensile testing: modulus, strength, elongation at break (neat matrix + composite)
- Barrier properties: gas permeability, water vapor transmission rate
- Thermal conductivity / electrical conductivity vs. filler loading (percolation curve)
- Melt rheology (for polymer NC): viscosity vs. shear rate, storage/loss modulus

## Reviewer risks

- Dispersion quality not quantified; "uniform dispersion" claimed from one TEM image at one location.
- Interfacial adhesion not characterized; no evidence of chemical bonding or mechanical interlocking.
- Property enhancement overclaimed vs. neat matrix with different processing history.
- Percolation threshold identified but critical exponent (β) not compared to theoretical models.
- Mechanical testing at only one strain rate or temperature; rate/time-dependent behavior ignored.

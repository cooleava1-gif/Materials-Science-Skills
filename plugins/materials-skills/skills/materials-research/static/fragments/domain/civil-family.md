# Domain: Civil and Construction Materials Family

Use for cross-domain civil and construction materials studies that span asphalt, cement, concrete, geotechnical, timber, waterproofing, steel-metal, and general construction material systems. This is the top-level family overview for civil engineering materials.

## Core logic

Civil/construction materials papers need to connect:

1. Material design or formulation selection.
2. Structural or mechanical performance under loading.
3. Durability under environmental exposure (moisture, temperature, chemical, cyclic).
4. Sustainability or life-cycle consideration.
5. Field application or constructability validation.

## Key evidence categories

- Mechanical: compressive, tensile, flexural, shear, modulus, fatigue.
- Durability: freeze-thaw, carbonation, chloride ingress, moisture damage, UV aging, corrosion.
- Microstructure: SEM, XRD, FTIR, TGA/DSC, MIP, CT scanning.
- Field data: in-situ testing, long-term monitoring, case studies, performance surveys.
- Sustainability: embodied carbon, recycled content, service life modeling, LCA.

## Reviewer risks

- Lab-to-field gap: accelerated lab tests do not reflect real service conditions.
- Missing durability evidence: only short-term mechanical data reported.
- Green-washing: sustainability claims unsupported by LCA or life-cycle data.
- Ignoring interaction effects: moisture, temperature, and loading act simultaneously in service.
- Overclaiming from single mix/proportion to general material behavior.
- Constructability overlooked: material works in lab but placement or curing is impractical in field.

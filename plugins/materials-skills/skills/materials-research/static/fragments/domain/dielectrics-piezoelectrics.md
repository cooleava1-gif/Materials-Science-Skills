# Domain: Dielectrics & Piezoelectrics

Covers capacitor ceramics (BaTiO3-based, SrTiO3), polymer dielectrics (PVDF, PI), piezoelectric sensors/actuators, and energy storage dielectrics.

## Core logic

1. Material selection and composition (acceptor/donor doping, polymer blending, nanofillers) set the polarization and breakdown field ceiling.
2. Processing (sintering temperature, poling conditions, film deposition) controls grain size, domain density, and defect concentration.
3. Polarization behavior (linear vs. relaxor vs. ferroelectric) determines energy storage mechanism: linear dielectrics have low loss but low energy density.
4. Dielectric and piezoelectric performance (εr, tan δ, d33, d31) must be reported across frequency and temperature ranges, not at a single point.
5. Temperature stability (Tc, depolarization temperature, capacitance variation with temperature) is critical for device reliability.
6. Hysteresis losses (P-E loop area, Q factor) must be quantified for high-power applications; low-field data do not predict high-field behavior.

## Key evidence categories

- εr and tan δ vs. frequency (1 Hz–1 MHz) and temperature (−50 to 300 °C)
- P-E hysteresis loops: Pr, Ec, maximum polarization, loop area (energy loss)
- Piezoelectric coefficients d33 (Berlincourt), d31 (impedance method)
- Curie temperature (Tc) from dielectric peak or DSC
- Impedance spectroscopy: resonance/anti-resonance for electromechanical coupling (k33, k31, kp)
- Breakdown field strength (for energy storage dielectrics, ≥20 specimens)

## Reviewer risks

- Frequency dispersion ignored; εr at 1 kHz reported but behavior at 1 MHz unknown (RF applicability unclear).
- Temperature stability not tested; capacitor-grade materials need <±15 % ΔC/C from −55 to 125 °C.
- Hysteresis losses not quantified; P-E loop shown but energy loss density not calculated.
- d33 measured only at room temperature; high-temperature piezoelectric applications need T-dependent data.
- Polymer dielectrics tested as thin films but bulk breakdown strength claimed.

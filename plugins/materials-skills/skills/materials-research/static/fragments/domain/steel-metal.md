# Domain: Steel and Metal Structures in Civil Engineering

Use for structural steel, reinforcement bars, prestressing steel, corrosion protection, coatings, and metal connections.

## Core logic

A steel/metal manuscript must define:

- Steel grade or alloy designation.
- Exposure or service environment (atmospheric, chloride, fire, fatigue).
- Processing, welding, or surface preparation.
- Mechanical or electrochemical test protocol.
- Service-life or performance boundary.

## Evidence contract

- Tensile properties: yield strength, ultimate strength, elongation (ASTM E8 / GB/T 228.1).
- Corrosion: mass loss, polarization, EIS, or coating adhesion (ASTM G1, G31, G5, D4541).
- Fire: temperature-time exposure and residual mechanical properties.
- Fatigue: stress range, R-ratio, crack initiation/propagation data.

## Reviewer risks

- Inferring long-term corrosion resistance from short immersion tests.
- Ignoring surface preparation and coating thickness in adhesion claims.
- Reporting weld performance without procedure or defect characterization.
- Extrapolating high-temperature behavior from room-temperature data.

# Domain: Civil Engineering Materials (General)

Use when the topic is civil/construction materials but does not fit a more specific subdomain.

## Core logic

A general civil-materials manuscript must define:

- Material system and function (structural, repair, protective, thermal, acoustic, durability, sustainability).
- Service environment and design life.
- Design variables, control group, and benchmark material.
- Test standard or custom test rationale.
- Performance-mechanism-application chain.

## Evidence contract

- Mechanical performance under representative service conditions.
- Durability evidence matching the claimed exposure.
- Sustainability claims supported by functional unit, reference material, and boundary.
- Clear construction or building application relevance.

## Reviewer risks

- The material is not clearly tied to a construction use case.
- Tests are generic and do not represent service conditions.
- The manuscript reads like material characterization without engineering significance.
- Sustainability is claimed from waste use alone.
- The study lacks a benchmark material.

## Subdomain triage

If the topic matches asphalt, cement/concrete, steel, geotechnical, timber/masonry, waterproofing, or sustainability/durability, prefer that subdomain fragment.

Use the more specific route when the scope is steel and metallic structures, geotechnical and rock-soil systems, timber/masonry assemblies, or waterproofing and sealants.

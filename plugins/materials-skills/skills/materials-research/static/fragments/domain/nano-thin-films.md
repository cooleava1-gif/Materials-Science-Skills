# Domain: Nano Thin Films

Covers thin films deposited by PVD (sputtering, evaporation), CVD (PECVD, LPCVD), ALD, and sol-gel for optical coatings, barriers, electronics, and protective layers.

## Core logic

1. Deposition parameters (pressure, temperature, power, precursor flow, substrate bias) control film structure (amorphous, nanocrystalline, epitaxial), stress, and composition.
2. Film thickness and uniformity (across wafer/substrate) must be measured by profilometry, ellipsometry, or cross-section SEM; nominal thickness alone is insufficient.
3. Substrate choice affects nucleation, adhesion, crystallographic texture, and residual stress; the substrate must be specified and its effect discussed.
4. Functional properties (optical constants n/k, electrical resistivity, hardness) depend on microstructure; structure-property linkage is essential.
5. Adhesion (scratch test, tape test, nanoindentation delamination) and environmental stability (humidity, temperature cycling) determine practical viability.
6. ALD films may show different properties than PVD/CVD counterparts due to conformality and density differences; method-specific data needed.

## Key evidence categories

- XRD: crystal phase, texture coefficient, grain size, residual stress (sin²ψ method)
- XPS: elemental composition, chemical state, depth profiling
- AFM: surface roughness (Rq, Ra), grain size on surface
- SEM cross-section: thickness, columnar/fibrous morphology, interface quality
- UV-Vis/NIR: transmittance, reflectance, optical bandgap (Tauc), n/k (Cauchy fit)
- Ellipsometry: thickness, optical constants (Ψ, Δ fitting)
- Four-point probe: sheet resistance, resistivity
- Adhesion test: critical load (Lc) from scratch test, ASTM D3359 tape test

## Reviewer risks

- Substrate effects ignored; film properties on glass vs. Si vs. polymer differ substantially.
- Thickness uniformity not reported; edge-to-center variation can exceed ±20 %.
- Environmental stability missing; no humidity, thermal cycling, or UV exposure data.
- Residual stress not measured; delamination or cracking in service attributed to "poor adhesion."
- Composition from XPS surface scan only; depth profiling needed for graded or interdiffused interfaces.

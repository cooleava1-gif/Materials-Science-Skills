# Domain: Functional Ceramics

Covers piezoelectrics (PZT, BaTiO3, KNbO3), ferroelectrics, low-temperature co-fired ceramics (LTCC), and dielectric ceramics for capacitors, sensors, actuators, and RF/microwave devices.

## Core logic

1. Composition and dopant selection (A/B-site substitution, morphotropic phase boundary engineering) determine accessible crystal phases and polarization states.
2. Sintering conditions control grain size, domain structure, and defect chemistry; oxygen vacancy concentration directly impacts losses.
3. Poling protocol (field strength, temperature, time) sets the piezoelectric coefficients; under-poling gives misleading d33.
4. Functional properties (d33, εr, tan δ, Tc, coupling coefficients) are frequency- and temperature-dependent; single-point values are incomplete.
5. Device integration requires co-firing compatibility (electrode, shrinkage matching) and long-term aging stability.
6. Domain wall contributions to permittivity and piezoelectric response must be separated from intrinsic lattice contributions.

## Key evidence categories

- XRD: phase purity, tetragonality (c/a ratio), Rietveld refinement
- SEM: grain size distribution, domain wall contrast, porosity
- Dielectric spectroscopy (εr, tan δ vs. frequency and temperature)
- P-E hysteresis loops (Pr, Ec, loop shape)
- Piezoelectric coefficient d33 (Berlincourt or interferometer)
- Impedance spectroscopy: resonance/anti-resonance for coupling factors

## Reviewer risks

- Composition-property phase diagram not referenced; claims near MPB without confirming phase coexistence.
- Aging and depolarization ignored; d33 measured only on freshly poled samples.
- Single-frequency dielectric measurement masks dispersion or relaxation phenomena.
- Lead-free compositions overclaimed against PZT benchmarks without comparable processing optimization.
- Grain size effects on Tc and d33 not discussed; nano-grain ceramics behave differently.

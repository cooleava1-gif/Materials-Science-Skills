# Domain: Nanomaterials

Use for nanoparticles, nanowires, nanotubes, thin films, 2D materials (graphene, MXenes, MoS2), nanocomposites, quantum dots, and nanostructured coatings. Covers synthesis, morphology control, surface chemistry, and application-level performance.

## Core logic

Nanomaterials papers need to connect:

1. Synthesis method and process parameters (precursor, temperature, solvent, time, capping agent).
2. Morphology and size control (particle size, shape, film thickness, layer number).
3. Surface chemistry and functionalization.
4. Property measurement at nanoscale (optical, electrical, mechanical, catalytic).
5. Application demonstration and scalability consideration.

## Key evidence categories

- Morphology: TEM, HRTEM, SEM, AFM, STEM-HAADF, particle size distribution.
- Size and surface: DLS, BET surface area, zeta potential, XPS.
- Structure: XRD (crystallite size, Scherrer), Raman, SAED.
- Optical/electronic: UV-Vis, PL (emission peak, quantum yield), I-V curves, impedance spectroscopy.
- Thermal/mechanical: TGA/DSC (thermal stability, decomposition), nanoindentation.
- Application: catalytic activity, sensing response, adsorption capacity, energy storage metrics.

## Reviewer risks

- Size distribution neglect: average size reported but polydispersity or distribution width omitted.
- Aggregation issues: pristine nanoparticles characterized but aggregated in application; no dispersion evidence.
- Missing reproducibility: synthesis repeated? batch-to-batch variation? not reported.
- Toxicology and safety: nanomaterials handling, environmental fate, and biocompatibility ignored.
- Surface area vs. activity mismatch: BET high but catalytic/sorption performance not proportional; mechanism unclear.
- Thin film thickness and uniformity: not measured or not uniform; property claims depend on thickness.
- Scalability gap: synthesis works at mg scale; no path to larger quantities discussed.

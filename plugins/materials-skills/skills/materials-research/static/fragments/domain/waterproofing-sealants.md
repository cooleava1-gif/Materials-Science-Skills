# Domain: Waterproofing and Sealant Materials

Use for bituminous membranes, liquid-applied membranes, construction sealants, waterstops, and polymer-modified repair layers.

## Core logic

A waterproofing/sealant manuscript must define:

- Product chemistry (bitumen, polyurethane, silicone, acrylic, etc.).
- Substrate type and surface preparation.
- Application method and thickness/coverage.
- Service exposure (water pressure, UV, thermal cycling, joint movement).
- Failure mode and acceptance criterion.

## Evidence contract

- Tensile strength and elongation at break (ASTM D412 / GB/T 328.9).
- Adhesion/pull-off strength to target substrate (ASTM D4541 / GB/T 16777).
- Water resistance and permeability under pressure or immersion.
- Accelerated aging (UV, heat, water immersion) when durability is claimed.

## Reviewer risks

- Calling a system field-ready without installation and aging constraints.
- Reporting laboratory adhesion without substrate and conditioning details.
- Ignoring joint movement capacity in sealant performance claims.
- Extrapolating membrane lifetime from single-factor aging tests.

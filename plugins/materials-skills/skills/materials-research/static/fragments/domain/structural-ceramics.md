# Domain: Structural Ceramics

Covers alumina (Al2O3), zirconia (ZrO2, Y-TZP), silicon carbide (SiC), and silicon nitride (Si3N4) used for wear parts, cutting tools, biomedical implants, and high-temperature structural components.

## Core logic

1. Powder synthesis route (solid-state, sol-gel, co-precipitation) determines particle size, phase purity, and sinterability.
2. Forming method (uniaxial/isostatic pressing, slip casting, injection molding) controls green density and defect population.
3. Sintering profile (temperature, atmosphere, hold time) governs densification, grain growth, and phase transformation (e.g., t→m ZrO2).
4. Relative density and grain size dictate mechanical performance: hardness, flexural strength, and fracture toughness.
5. Toughening mechanisms (transformation toughening, crack bridging, grain pull-out) must be linked to composition/microstructure.
6. Reliability assessment requires Weibull statistics on strength data; single-point values are insufficient.
7. Surface finish and machining damage affect measured strength and must be reported.

## Key evidence categories

- Density / porosity (Archimedes, mercury porosimetry)
- Vickers / Knoop hardness
- Fracture toughness (SENB, IF, SEVNB) with method stated
- Flexural strength (3- or 4-point bend, ≥30 specimens for Weibull)
- XRD phase identification and quantification
- SEM: grain size (intercept method), fracture mode (inter/transgranular), secondary phases

## Reviewer risks

- Sintering temperature sensitivity ignored; small shifts (±20 °C) can drastically change density/grain size.
- Weibull modulus not reported; strength claims based on <10 specimens.
- Single-property reporting (hardness only, no toughness) hides trade-offs.
- Transformation toughening in ZrO2 not validated by XRD or Raman on fractured surfaces.
- Processing defects (pores, agglomerates) not quantified; polished-section only.

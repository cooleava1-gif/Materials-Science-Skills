# Domain: Thermosets

## Usage

Covers epoxy, phenolic, polyimide, bismaleimide (BMI), cyanate ester, and vinyl ester resin
systems. Applicable to aerospace composites, adhesives, coatings, electronic encapsulants,
and structural applications requiring dimensional stability at elevated temperature.

## Core logic

1. Resin/hardener formulation (stoichiometry, catalysts, modifiers) determines pot life and reactivity window.
2. Curing schedule (temperature ramp, hold time, pressure) controls degree of cure and residual stress.
3. Crosslink density, derived from cure chemistry, sets Tg ceiling, solvent resistance, and modulus.
4. Thermal and mechanical performance (Tg, flexural strength, interlaminar shear) are validated at operational temperature range.
5. Long-term aging (thermo-oxidative, hydrolytic, UV) and degradation kinetics define service life.

## Key evidence categories

- Cure kinetics: DSC (degree of cure, enthalpy, Tg), rheometer (gel point, viscosity build)
- Thermal: DMA (storage modulus, tan delta peak = Tg), TGA (thermal stability, char yield)
- Chemical: FTIR (epoxide index, disappearance of cure bands), NIR for inline cure monitoring
- Mechanical: tensile, flexural, ILSS, impact (Charpy/Izod)
- Morphology: SEM (fracture surfaces, phase separation in toughened systems)
- Durability: accelerated aging (heat, moisture), solvent exposure weight change

## Reviewer risks

- Degree of cure not verified; DSC data absent or only initial Tg reported without confirming full cure.
- Post-cure effects ignored; Tg reported for under-cured specimens that would shift with proper post-cure schedule.
- Tg claimed from DSC inflection only, not corroborated by DMA tan delta peak, leading to overestimated service temperature.
- Stoichiometric ratio of resin to hardener not reported or deviates from supplier optimum without justification.
- Toughener or modifier content undisclosed; mechanical gains attributed to base resin chemistry alone.

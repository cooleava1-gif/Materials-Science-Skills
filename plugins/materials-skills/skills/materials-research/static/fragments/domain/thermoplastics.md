# Domain: Thermoplastics

## Usage

Covers polyethylene (PE, HDPE, LDPE), polypropylene (PP), polyethylene terephthalate (PET),
nylon (PA6, PA66), polycarbonate (PC), polyether ether ketone (PEEK), and related
melt-processable polymers. Applicable to packaging, automotive, structural, and biomedical
applications where reprocessability is a design requirement.

## Core logic

1. Synthesis/polymerization route determines molecular weight distribution and chain architecture.
2. Molecular weight (Mw, Mn) and crystallinity govern mechanical and thermal performance ceilings.
3. Processing method (injection molding, extrusion, blow molding, thermoforming) sets cooling rate, residual stress, and skin-core morphology.
4. Mechanical response (tensile, impact, creep) and thermal limits (HDT, continuous use temperature) are validated against application requirements.
5. Recycling strategy (mechanical, chemical, energy recovery) and end-of-life pathway close the sustainability loop.

## Key evidence categories

- Molecular weight: GPC (Mw, Mn, PDI), intrinsic viscosity
- Thermal: DSC (Tg, Tm, crystallinity), TGA (degradation onset), DMA (storage/loss modulus)
- Mechanical: tensile (strength, modulus, elongation), impact (Izod, Charpy), flexural
- Rheological: melt flow index (MFI), capillary rheometry
- Chemical: FTIR (functional groups, degradation products), Raman
- Morphology: SEM (fracture surfaces, spherulites), WAXD/SAXS (crystalline structure)

## Reviewer risks

- Molecular weight distribution (PDI) ignored; only Mw reported despite PDI-sensitive processing behavior.
- Processing conditions (melt temperature, mold temperature, cooling rate) not documented, preventing reproducibility.
- Recyclability and end-of-life data absent; claims of "recyclable material" unsupported by degradation cycling evidence.
- Crystallinity measured by DSC only; no cross-validation by XRD when processing-induced orientation is significant.
- Impact data reported at room temperature only; ductile-brittle transition temperature not identified.

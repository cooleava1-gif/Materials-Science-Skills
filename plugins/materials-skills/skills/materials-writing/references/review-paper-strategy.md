# Review Paper Strategy

A mini-review should be narrow, structured, and opinionated enough to be useful. The default structure must be synthesis axes, not a paper list.

## Synthesis-first rule

Organize the review by synthesis axes:

- **Mechanism** — how the modification or phenomenon works (e.g., curing, crosslinking, interfacial bonding).
- **Material design** — formulation variables, modifiers, and processing routes.
- **Performance trade-off** — gain in one property vs. loss in another.
- **Research agenda** — what experiments must resolve next.

Do not organize by paper-by-paper summaries. A paper-by-paper structure makes the review easy to write but hard to use; it hides the field-level patterns the reader needs.

## Outline structure

1. **Scope and problem** — define the material system and the engineering problem.
2. **Synthesis axes** — present the thematic dimensions and justify why they were chosen.
3. **Thematic sections** — for each axis, synthesize what is known, what is contested, and what is missing.
4. **Knowledge gap** — identify the evidence gap that blocks better material design.
5. **Research agenda** — propose specific experiments, methods, or comparisons that would close the gap.

## Evidence quality checklist

For each study cited, record:

- Material system and formulation.
- Test method and standard (if any).
- Conditions (temperature, humidity, aging, scale).
- Key result with units.
- Mechanism evidence (if claimed).

## Recommended taxonomy for waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt

- material components,
- emulsification and curing behavior,
- bonding-performance tests,
- durability and service conditioning,
- characterization evidence,
- construction workability,
- standards and field gaps.

The research agenda should identify what future experiments must resolve, such as dosage optimization under wet conditions, mechanism-performance coupling, and standard-compatible tack coat evaluation.

## Scope rule

For a master's-stage mini-review, keep the scope narrow enough to be deep: for example, waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt tack coat bonding and durability rather than all modified asphalt emulsions.

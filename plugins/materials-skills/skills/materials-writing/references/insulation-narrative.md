# Thermal Insulation Narrative

For thermal insulation manuscripts, the strongest narrative arc is usually:

1. building energy consumption accounts for X% of total energy use, and thermal insulation is the most direct strategy for reducing heating and cooling demand,
2. current insulation materials face a trade-off between thermal performance and mechanical integrity, or between low conductivity and moisture durability,
3. this paper develops [material system: aerogel, foam, VIP, PCM composite] that achieves [target conductivity] while maintaining [mechanical property],
4. we systematically evaluate the effect of [density, porosity, composition, processing] on thermal conductivity, mechanical properties, and durability,
5. the results demonstrate that [optimum formulation] balances performance across all three requirements, suitable for [application scenario].

## Key evidence chain

- Formulation → pore structure → thermal conductivity → mechanical strength → durability → application suitability.
- Each application claim must be supported by the appropriate service-condition test.
- Moisture effect must be quantified before building application can be claimed.

## Common section structure

- Introduction: energy context → insulation limits → specific gap (mechanical/thermal/moisture trade-off) → approach.
- Methods: raw materials → fabrication → density/porosity characterization → thermal measurement (standard, T_mean) → mechanical → durability.
- Results: first porosity/pore structure, then thermal, then mechanical, then durability (if any).
- Discussion: benchmark against commercial insulation materials; discuss trade-offs; identify application window.
- Conclusions: report lambda value with test T_mean; note moisture sensitivity if present.

## Useful keywords

thermal conductivity, lambda, R-value, U-value, ASTM C518, guarded hot plate, heat flow meter, mean temperature, thermal bridge, aerogel, xerogel, cryogel, VIP, fumed silica, MF, PU, phenolic foam, mineral wool, glass wool, EPS, XPS, vacuum insulation panel, phase change material, PCM, hygrothermal, moisture uptake.

## Reviewer-safe language

- "The thermal conductivity was 0.028 W/(m.K) at a mean temperature of 25 C" (always report temperature).
- "The material shows promise for building insulation applications, pending verification of long-term hygrothermal performance."
- "Compared to commercial silica aerogel (lambda ~0.015), our material offers improved mechanical strength at the cost of higher conductivity (0.028 vs 0.015)."
- Do not claim "superinsulating" unless lambda < 0.020 W/(m.K) at relevant conditions.

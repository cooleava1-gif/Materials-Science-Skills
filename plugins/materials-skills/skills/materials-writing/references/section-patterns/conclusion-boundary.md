# Conclusion Boundary

Use this section-level factory to make materials conclusions strong without
claiming beyond the evidence.

## Move 1: contribution

Purpose: state the contribution as a specific property-mechanism-service link.

Sentence pattern:

- `This study demonstrates that [material variable] can improve [property] within [composition/process window] for [application].`
- `The main contribution is a linked evaluation of [performance metric] and [mechanism evidence], which clarifies [bounded structure-property relation].`

## Move 2: limitation

Purpose: name the missing condition before the reviewer does.

Sentence pattern:

- `The conclusion is limited by [sample scale/test duration/standard/lack of field validation].`
- `Because [characterization/test] was not included, [specific mechanism] should be regarded as a supported interpretation rather than a confirmed cause.`

## Move 3: next step

Purpose: define the next experiment that would strengthen the claim.

Sentence pattern:

- `Future work should verify [durability/service condition] using [standard/protocol] and [field/larger-scale sample].`
- `Additional [characterization method] is needed to distinguish [mechanism A] from [alternative mechanism].`

## Move 4: prevent overclaim

Purpose: avoid universal language.

Sentence pattern:

- `Within the tested [composition/process/aging] range, [material] shows potential for [bounded application].`
- `The results support [claim strength] rather than proving [unsupported universal claim].`

## Overclaim replacements

- Replace `proves the mechanism` with `supports a mechanism in which [evidence-bounded explanation]`.
- Replace `solves the durability problem` with `improves [durability metric] under [tested protocol]`.
- Replace `can be widely applied` with `is promising for [application] after [missing validation] is addressed`.

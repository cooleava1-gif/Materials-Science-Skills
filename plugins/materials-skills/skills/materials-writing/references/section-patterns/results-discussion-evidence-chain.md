# Results Discussion Evidence Chain

Use this section-level factory to keep Results and Discussion grounded in
observable evidence before mechanism interpretation.

## Move 1: observation

Purpose: report what changed without explaining it too early.

Sentence pattern:

- `As shown in Fig. [x], [metric] changed from [value] to [value] when [variable] increased from [level] to [level].`
- `[Image/spectrum/curve] shows [observable feature], especially at [condition/composition].`

## Move 2: quantitative result

Purpose: quantify effect size and uncertainty.

Sentence pattern:

- `The maximum [property] reached [value] at [condition], corresponding to a [percentage] change relative to [control].`
- `The difference between [group A] and [group B] was [statistical descriptor], indicating [bounded performance trend].`

## Move 3: mechanism evidence

Purpose: interpret only after naming the characterization support.

Sentence pattern:

- `[FTIR/XRD/SEM/DSC/TGA/rheology evidence] suggests that [structural/chemical feature] is associated with [mechanism].`
- `This interpretation is supported by [evidence 1] and [evidence 2], which jointly indicate [bounded mechanism].`

## Move 4: alternative explanation exclusion

Purpose: show what else could explain the result and how far the data can
exclude it.

Sentence pattern:

- `A possible alternative explanation is [alternative], but [control/test/characterization] reduces this possibility because [evidence].`
- `Because [missing evidence] was not measured, the data support an association between [feature] and [property] rather than proving [causal mechanism].`

## Discussion boundary

Do not jump from one performance curve to a molecular mechanism. Require at
least one mechanism evidence anchor or write the claim as a hypothesis.

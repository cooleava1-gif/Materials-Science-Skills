# Method Pre-Writing Questions

`Before writing Method, answer: (1) what characterization techniques exist, (2) what question each answers, (3) what standards the performance tests cite, (4) what the statistical plan is.`

```text
Questions:
(1) What characterization techniques are used in this study?
(2) For each technique, answer three questions:
    - What specific feature does it probe (peak, phase, morphology, transition)?
    - What question does it answer (chemistry, structure, mechanism)?
    - What parameters must be reported for reproducibility?
(3) What standards (ASTM, EN, ISO, GB/T, JIS) do the performance tests cite?
    - Are the standards actually followed, or adapted? State modifications.
(4) What is the statistical plan?
    - Number of replicates per group.
    - Reported descriptor (mean ± SD, median, CI).
    - Test used for comparison (t-test, ANOVA) and significance level.
```

Recommended action:

1. Organize answers in a table (technique × purpose × parameters) before writing paragraphs.
2. Cross-check that every performance trend in your Results has at least one characterization technique assigned to explain it.

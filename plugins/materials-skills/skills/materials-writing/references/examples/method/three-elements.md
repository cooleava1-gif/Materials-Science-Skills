# The Three Elements of a Materials Method

`Materials methods rest on three elements: synthesis-protocol, characterization-purpose, performance-test. Distilled from methods.md fragment and the three-part skeleton.`

## Element 1: synthesis-protocol

Answers: how was the material made, in enough detail to reproduce?

Required content:
- Precursors: source, purity/specification, pretreatment.
- Formulation: composition in precise units (wt%, phr, mol%, ratio).
- Processing parameters: temperature, time, pressure, atmosphere, cooling rate.
- Standard or adapted protocol citation.

Checklist:
- [ ] Could a researcher in the same field remake your material from this text alone?
- [ ] Are all variables that affect the result reported (not just the ones you varied)?
- [ ] Is the protocol cited or adapted with the modifications stated?

## Element 2: characterization-purpose

Answers: what question does each characterization technique answer?

Required content:
- Instrument model and parameters.
- The specific feature being probed (peak, phase, morphology, transition).
- The purpose sentence: "to monitor / to determine / to distinguish [feature]".

Checklist:
- [ ] Does each technique have an explicit purpose, not just "X was performed"?
- [ ] Are parameters (range, scans, resolution) sufficient to reproduce the measurement?
- [ ] See `characterization-purpose-library.md` for per-technique purpose templates.

## Element 3: performance-test

Answers: what was measured, under what condition, with what statistical backing?

Required content:
- Test standard (ASTM, EN, ISO, GB/T, JIS) or detailed protocol.
- Specimen geometry, dimensions, and number of replicates.
- Test condition: temperature, humidity, aging state, loading rate.
- Statistical method and software.

Checklist:
- [ ] Is the standard number correct and actually followed?
- [ ] Are replicates and the statistical treatment reported?
- [ ] Is the test condition specified (a strength without a condition is not a result)?

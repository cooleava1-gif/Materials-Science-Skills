# Abstract Template A — Performance Breakthrough

`Use when one property jumps clearly past a stated ceiling under a stated condition. Distilled from abstract-claim-arc.md Move 1-5 and published-article-patterns Pattern A/B/C.`

```latex
% Move 1: background pain — name the material and the specific bottleneck, not the whole field
[材料名] is increasingly used for [应用场景], but [性能瓶颈] still limits [服役条件或工程用途].

% Move 2: current ceiling — give the number that defines the ceiling
Current [材料家族] typically achieve [性能指标] of [当前天花板数值] under [测试条件], leaving little margin for [工程需求].

% Move 3: this design — one sentence, name the route
Here, we prepared [材料变体] by [制备路线] and evaluated [性能集合] under [标准号/条件].

% Move 4: breakthrough result — dosage + value + comparison + condition, all in one sentence
The [优化配比] group reached [性能数值] ± [不确定度] [单位], [提升百分比] higher than the control ([对照数值]) under [测试条件].

% Move 5: mechanism evidence — one line, hedged verb
[表征技术] evidence suggests that [结构/化学特征] is associated with [机制], which helps explain the [性能变化].

% Move 6: application boundary — close with the boundary, not a universal claim
These results identify [配比/工艺窗口] as a promising route for [应用] under [测试条件], while [未验证的耐久性/规模化问题] remains to be verified.
```

## Usage

- Replace every `[...]` placeholder with your specific material, number, and condition.
- Move 4 must contain a real number from your data — never invent a percentage.
- The hedging verb in Move 5 (`suggests`, `is consistent with`) must match your evidence strength.

## Anti-overclaim check

- Do NOT write: `[材料] proves a new mechanism for all [应用].`
- Write instead: `[证据] supports a mechanism in which [bounded explanation] under the tested [配比/条件] range.`
- Do NOT omit the test condition in Move 4 — a percentage without a condition is an overclaim.

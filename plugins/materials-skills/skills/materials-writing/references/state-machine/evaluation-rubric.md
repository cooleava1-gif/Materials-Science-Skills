# Evaluation Rubric

Score each dimension from 0 to 10. Use the anchors to prevent score inflation:
3 is weak, 5 is usable only as a rough internal draft, 7 is supervisor-facing,
and 9 is near submission polish. Route the next revision by the lowest score.

## Dimensions

### task_scope_clarity
- Anchor 3: Task type, reader, section, and deliverable are unclear.
- Anchor 5: Task is named, but boundaries and exclusions are incomplete.
- Anchor 7: Scope, audience, deliverable, and blocked claims are explicit.
- Anchor 9: Scope is stable across state, foundation files, and output.

### scientific_tension
- Anchor 3: No clear gap, contradiction, or materials problem is visible.
- Anchor 5: A broad gap is stated, but its scientific pressure is generic.
- Anchor 7: Field progress, unresolved gap, and material consequence align.
- Anchor 9: Tension is precise, consequential, and section-ready.

### evidence_match
- Anchor 3: Major claims lack source text, data, figure, table, or record support.
- Anchor 5: Some claims have evidence, but unsupported claims remain mixed in.
- Anchor 7: Major claims map to evidence and evidence strength is named.
- Anchor 9: Evidence, claim strength, and section placement are fully aligned.

### materials_method_reproducibility
- Anchor 3: Methods, materials, standards, or conditions are missing.
- Anchor 5: Core method exists, but replicate, statistics, or conditioning details are thin.
- Anchor 7: Materials, preparation, standards, conditions, and statistics are usable.
- Anchor 9: Reproducibility is reviewer-ready for the target journal family.

### argument_chain
- Anchor 3: Paragraphs are descriptive, disconnected, or carry multiple messages.
- Anchor 5: Section order is plausible, but transitions and evidence logic are weak.
- Anchor 7: Paragraph messages support the one-sentence argument in order.
- Anchor 9: The full chain moves cleanly from problem to evidence to boundary.

### innovation_specificity
- Anchor 3: Novelty language is vague, inflated, or unsupported.
- Anchor 5: Contribution is present but not separated from prior work.
- Anchor 7: Innovation is specific to material design, method, evidence, or mechanism.
- Anchor 9: Contribution is precise, bounded, and journal-positioned.

### risk_boundary
- Anchor 3: Durability, mechanism, comparator, citation, or application risks are hidden.
- Anchor 5: Some risks are named, but claims still exceed evidence.
- Anchor 7: Main limitations and reviewer risks are visible and bounded.
- Anchor 9: Risks are integrated without weakening the paper's argument.

### language_quality
- Anchor 3: Language obscures claims, evidence, or section purpose.
- Anchor 5: Readable, but generic phrasing or translation residue remains.
- Anchor 7: Clear manuscript prose with stable terminology and paragraph focus.
- Anchor 9: Concise, field-appropriate, and ready for final polishing.

## Thresholds

| Use case | Minimum average | Blocking rule |
|---|---:|---|
| Foundation readiness | 7.5 | No dimension below 6.5 |
| Section draft | 6.5 | Evidence match and risk boundary must be at least 6.0 |
| Manuscript/proposal review | 7.0 | No unsupported major claim |
| Final polish handoff | 8.0 | No dimension below 7.0 |

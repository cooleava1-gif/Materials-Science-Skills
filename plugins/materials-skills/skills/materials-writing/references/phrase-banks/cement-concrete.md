# Cement and Concrete Phrase Bank

Use this bank for cement paste, concrete, mortar, supplementary cementitious
materials (SCM), alkali-activated materials, and cementitious composite
manuscripts.

---

## phrase-bank

### hydration

- `The [XRD/TGA/SEM] results indicate that [SCM/dosage] altered the hydration product assemblage, with [increased/decreased] [portlandite/C–S–H/ettringite] content at [age].`
- `The degree of hydration, estimated from [method], reached [value]% at [age] for the [mix design], suggesting [accelerated/delayed] reaction kinetics.`
- `The pozzolanic reaction between [SCM] and portlandite is supported by [CH reduction/C–S–H formation], but the rate depends on [curing temperature/SCM fineness/alkali content].`

### compressive strength

- `The [mix] achieved a compressive strength of [value] MPa at [age] ([standard]), representing a [percentage] change relative to [reference mix].`
- `Strength development beyond [age] suggests continued [hydration/pozzolanic reaction], which should be confirmed by [XRD/TGA/porosity] evidence.`
- `The strength reduction at [high dosage/condition] may be attributed to [increased porosity/delayed hydration/microcracking], but direct [MIP/SEM] evidence is needed.`

### workability

- `The slump of [mix] measured [value] mm ([standard]), indicating [acceptable/reduced/excessive] workability for [application].`
- `Workability loss with [admixture/SCM] is consistent with [water demand/setting time/particle morphology], as supported by [rheology/flow table/V-funnel] results.`

### durability — chloride ingress

- `The chloride diffusion coefficient of [mix] was [value] × 10⁻¹² m²/s ([standard]), indicating [improved/reduced] resistance to chloride ingress relative to [reference].`
- `The reduced chloride penetration depth ([value] mm at [age]) is consistent with [pore refinement/C–S–H binding/aluminate phase formation], but field exposure conditions differ from the accelerated test.`

### durability — carbonation

- `After [duration] exposure to [CO₂ concentration/condition], the carbonation depth of [mix] was [value] mm, [higher/lower] than [reference mix].`
- `Carbonation resistance should be assessed together with [porosity/permeability/cover depth] because carbonation rate depends on both material and design parameters.`

### supplementary cementitious materials

- `Replacing [percentage]% of Portland cement with [SCM] [increased/decreased] the [age] strength by [value]%, while [reducing/increasing] the heat of hydration.`
- `The optimum SCM replacement level is [value]%, beyond which [strength reduction/delayed setting/increased porosity] becomes significant.`
- `The synergy between [SCM A] and [SCM B] is suggested by [strength/durability/hydration] data, but the mechanism requires [TGA/XRD/MIP] evidence.`

### alkali-activated materials

- `The alkali-activated [slag/fly ash/metakaolin] binder achieved [strength] MPa at [age] with [activator type/concentration], comparable to [OPC reference].`
- `The reaction product identified as [C–A–S–H/N–A–S–H/hybrid] is consistent with [XRD/FTIR/NMR] evidence, though the long-term stability under [condition] remains uncertain.`

### fiber reinforcement

- `Adding [fiber type] at [volume fraction]% increased the [flexural/tensile/impact] strength by [value]%, with crack-bridging observed in [SEM/photography].`
- `The fiber–matrix interface bond is supported by [pull-out/IFSS/microscopy] evidence, but the contribution of [fiber orientation/distribution/alignment] should be quantified.`

### shrinkage and cracking

- `The autogenous/dry shrinkage of [mix] was [value] με at [age], [higher/lower] than [reference], consistent with [porosity/relative humidity/SCM reaction].`
- `Crack initiation was observed at [condition/stress level], and the cracking risk should be assessed together with [restraint/thermal gradient/early-age strength development].`

---

## reviewer-red-flags

- Claiming strength improvement without reporting the mix design, curing condition, age, and test standard.
- Reporting durability improvement from a single accelerated test without discussing field relevance.
- Attributing strength changes to hydration without XRD, TGA, SEM, or calorimetry evidence.
- Comparing SCM mixes at different ages without acknowledging the effect of continued hydration.
- Claiming "green concrete" or "sustainable cement" without LCA, embodied carbon, or functional unit definition.
- Reporting fiber reinforcement without fiber distribution, orientation, or interface evidence.

## safe-claim-patterns

- `Within the tested [replacement level/curing condition/age] range, [mix] shows [strength/durability] improvement consistent with [mechanism evidence].`
- `The [chloride/carbonation/freeze-thaw] resistance of [mix] is promising for [application], but long-term field exposure data are needed to confirm service performance.`
- `The results suggest that [SCM/fiber/admixture] contributes to [property] through [mechanism], as supported by [characterization], within the tested [condition] boundary.`

# WER-EA Phrase Bank

Use this bank for waterborne epoxy resin modified emulsified asphalt (WER-EA)
manuscripts and reviews.

## phrase-bank

### bonding

- `The bonding strength of [WER-EA formulation] increased from [value] to [value] after [curing condition], indicating improved interface resistance under [test standard].`
- `The pull-off/shear result suggests that [dosage] enhances bonding at the [substrate/asphalt] interface, but the claim is limited to [temperature/moisture condition].`

### demulsification

- `The demulsification process was accelerated/delayed by [component], as reflected by [observable metric], which may affect film formation before epoxy curing is complete.`
- `Because demulsification and curing occur concurrently, the observed bonding change should be interpreted as a coupled effect of [water evaporation/emulsion breaking] and [epoxy network formation].`

### curing

- `The change in [FTIR peak/DSC transition/rheological index] suggests progressive curing of the epoxy phase during [curing time/temperature].`
- `The curing-related interpretation is supported by [characterization], rather than inferred solely from the increase in bonding strength.`

### interfacial energy

- `The decrease/increase in calculated interfacial energy indicates a more favorable interaction between [phase A] and [phase B], consistent with the measured [bonding metric].`
- `Interfacial energy analysis supports a thermodynamic tendency for [wetting/adhesion], but it does not by itself prove long-term bonding durability.`

### emulsion stability

- `The storage stability index changed by [value], suggesting that [emulsifier/epoxy dosage] affects emulsion stability before construction application.`
- `A stable emulsion is necessary for uniform spraying, but excessive stability may delay demulsification and reduce early bonding development.`

## reviewer-red-flags

- Claiming that bonding improvement proves chemical reaction without FTIR, DSC, rheology, or comparable characterization.
- Ignoring the coupled timing of demulsification, water evaporation, and epoxy curing.
- Reporting bonding strength without substrate condition, curing time, temperature, moisture state, or test standard.
- Treating emulsion stability as always beneficial without discussing construction demulsification.

## safe-claim-patterns

- `The data support an association between [curing/demulsification indicator] and [bonding metric], while direct causal confirmation requires [additional characterization/control].`
- `Within the tested [dosage] range, [formulation] provides a workable balance between emulsion stability and bonding development.`
- `The proposed mechanism is consistent with [FTIR/DSC/SEM/rheology evidence] and should be interpreted within the [curing condition/substrate/test standard] used here.`

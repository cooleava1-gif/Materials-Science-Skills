# Polymer Composites Phrase Bank

Use this bank for fiber-reinforced polymers, laminated composites, particulate
polymer composites, and hybrid polymer systems.

## phrase-bank

### interfacial bonding

- `Improved interfacial bonding between [fiber/filler] and [matrix] is suggested by [SEM fracture surface/IFSS/ILSS/DMA] evidence.`
- `The increase in [strength/modulus] is associated with better load transfer at the interface, but direct confirmation requires [interfacial test/chemical analysis].`

### fiber orientation

- `Fiber orientation analysis shows preferential alignment along [direction], which explains the anisotropic response in [tensile/flexural/fatigue] loading.`
- `The effect of fiber orientation should be interpreted together with [fiber volume fraction/void content/processing condition].`

### interlaminar shear

- `The interlaminar shear strength increased from [value] to [value], indicating improved resistance to delamination under [test standard].`
- `Because ILSS is sensitive to voids and laminate quality, [microscopy/void fraction] is needed to support the interface interpretation.`

### fatigue

- `After [number] fatigue cycles at [stress ratio/load level], [composite] retained [percentage] of [property], suggesting [bounded fatigue resistance].`
- `The fatigue trend is consistent with [crack deflection/fiber bridging/interface debonding] observed in [fractography method].`

### fracture mechanism

- `Fractography indicates [fiber pull-out/matrix cracking/interface debonding], which supports a fracture mechanism dominated by [load-transfer/fiber bridging/delamination].`
- `The fracture mechanism should be reported as evidence-supported interpretation unless in situ crack growth or quantitative fracture analysis is available.`

## reviewer-red-flags

- Claiming stronger interface only from tensile strength without SEM, ILSS, IFSS, DMA, or fracture evidence.
- Ignoring fiber orientation, void content, and volume fraction when comparing composite performance.
- Treating fatigue improvement as proven by static strength.
- Reporting fracture mechanism without representative fractography or crack-path evidence.

## safe-claim-patterns

- `The results suggest improved load transfer at the [fiber/filler]-[matrix] interface, as supported by [evidence], within the tested [loading/process] condition.`
- `The fatigue claim is limited to [cycle count/stress ratio/environment] and should not be generalized to service spectra without further validation.`
- `The observed fracture features are consistent with [mechanism], but alternative contributions from [voids/orientation/residual stress] cannot be fully excluded.`

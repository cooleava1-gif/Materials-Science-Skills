# Journal: Cement and Concrete Composites (CCC) — writing

## Read the shared facts first

Open `../../_shared/journal-formats/ccc.md` for the authoritative formatting facts: article types and word limits, abstract rules, figure specs, reference style, mandatory statements, and common desk-rejection patterns.

The notes below are the **drafting action layer** on top of those facts.

## Audience and scope

CCC readers expect composite science, not mix optimization. The work must advance cement/concrete composites through mechanism depth and insight beyond property improvement. Asphalt-only work routes to CBM, RMPD, or IJPE.

## Drafting priorities

- **Composite contribution must be central.** Frame the paper around the composite logic — why these components together — not around a single-component property.
- **Foreground the interface.** Fiber/matrix, filler/binder, or paste/aggregate interfaces are the heart of CCC papers. Each performance claim should connect to interfacial evidence.
- **Fiber and supplementary-cementitious-material (SCM) synergy needs explicit evidence.** Do not assert synergy from a single property; show the interaction (e.g., crack-bridging + pore-refinement together).
- **Microstructure evidence is expected, not optional.** SEM, XRD, TG, or MIP must back mechanism claims. Performance changes attributed to a mechanism without microstructure support are a desk-rejection trigger.
- **Connect mechanical behavior and durability.** Isolated property measurement reads as incomplete; show how the composite behaves under more than one demand.

## Word and figure budget (research article)

Typical 6,000–10,000-word band. CCC papers tend toward the higher end because of mechanism depth:

| Section | Suggested budget |
|---|---|
| Introduction | ~1,200 |
| Methods | ~1,800 |
| Results & Discussion | ~4,000 |
| Conclusion | ~500 |

Plan figure budget around microstructure panels (SEM, XRD, TG) plus performance plots; do not let microstructure figures crowd out the performance narrative.

## Desk-rejection patterns to pre-empt

From the shared facts file: no composite contribution, isolated property study, asphalt-only scope, and missing microstructure evidence. Before drafting, check:

- [ ] Is the composite contribution stated as the central advance, not as a side note?
- [ ] Is interfacial or microstructure evidence tied to each mechanism claim?
- [ ] If synergy is claimed, is the interaction shown across more than one property?
- [ ] Is the work cement/concrete composite science, not pure mix optimization?

## Cover letter

State in three sentences: the composite advance, the mechanism insight, and why it matters to the cement/concrete composites community.

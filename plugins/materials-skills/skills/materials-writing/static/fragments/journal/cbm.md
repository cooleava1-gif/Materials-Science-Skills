# Journal: Construction and Building Materials (CBM) — writing

## Read the shared facts first

Open `../../_shared/journal-formats/cbm.md` for the authoritative formatting facts: article types and word limits, abstract rules, figure specs, reference style, mandatory statements, and common desk-rejection patterns.

The notes below are the **drafting action layer** on top of those facts.

## Audience and scope

CBM targets the construction-materials community. Readers expect practical material value, complete testing, and explicit comparison with existing construction materials. Pure chemistry or pavement design without a material contribution routes elsewhere.

## Drafting priorities

- **Experimental papers are the default.** Lead with the material system, the property tested, and the construction-relevant outcome.
- **Performance data must carry statistics.** Report mean ± SD (or SE), replicate count, and the test used. `Significant` only with a stated test and p-value.
- **Durability claims need long-term data or an explicit boundary.** If only short-term data exists, state the conditioning and extrapolation limit; do not generalize to service life.
- **Cite the test standard for every property.** ASTM, EN, ISO, GB/T, or JIS — unnamed methods are a desk-rejection trigger.
- **Foreground the comparator.** Improvement means nothing without a named baseline material or mix.

## Word and figure budget (research article)

No strict cap, but plan against the typical 6,000–10,000-word band:

| Section | Suggested budget |
|---|---|
| Introduction | ~1,000 |
| Methods | ~1,800 |
| Results & Discussion | ~3,500 |
| Conclusion | ~500 |

Keep figures focused; CBM tolerates more figures than Nature-family venues, but each must earn its place.

## Desk-rejection patterns to pre-empt

From the shared facts file, the most common CBM desk rejections are: no construction-material relevance, single-property study, missing control group, weak mechanism evidence, and unsolicited reviews. Before drafting, check:

- [ ] Is the construction-material relevance stated in the first paragraph of the introduction?
- [ ] Is there a named control or baseline for every improvement claim?
- [ ] Is at least one characterization technique (SEM, XRD, FTIR, TGA, etc.) tied to each mechanism claim?
- [ ] Are durability claims bounded by the actual conditioning window?

## Cover letter

Editors triage on the cover letter. State in three sentences: what the finding is, what is novel, and why it matters to the construction-materials community.

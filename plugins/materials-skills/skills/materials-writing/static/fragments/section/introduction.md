# Introduction

> **Domain context**: The `domain` axis has loaded domain-specific writing guidance for [detected domain]. The ladder below is the general structure; the domain guide contains domain-specific gap types, citation strategy, and reviewer expectations. Use both together.

Build the introduction as a gap ladder, not a topic funnel. Each paragraph must move the reader one step closer to the evidence gap this paper fills.

## Paragraph sequence

1. **Field progress** — what has been established in the field and why the topic matters.
2. **Contradiction or bottleneck** — the performance ceiling, processing barrier, durability gap, or unresolved trade-off that limits current materials.
3. **Evidence gap** — what specific evidence is missing. The gap must name a missing measurement, comparison, condition, or mechanism link, not just a missing topic.
4. **paper entry** — what this study contributes and how it closes the gap.

## Gap-ladder examples tied to missing evidence

- Weak: "Few studies have investigated waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt."
- Stronger: "While dry bonding strength of WER-EA tack coats has been reported, wet bonding retention after freeze-thaw conditioning and the corresponding interface morphology evolution remain unquantified."

- Weak: "The mechanical properties of polymer composites need improvement."
- Stronger: "The relationship between crosslink density and creep compliance in this epoxy system has not been measured above the glass-transition temperature, so thermal-mechanical trade-offs cannot be quantified."

# Cover Letter

Use this fragment when the user asks for a journal submission cover letter.

## Structure

1. **Editor address and date**
2. **Salutation** — "Dear Editor," or "Dear Dr. [Name]," if known
3. **Submission statement** — Title, manuscript type, and journal name
4. **Core contribution** — One paragraph summarizing the problem, approach, and key finding
5. **Fit to journal** — One sentence explaining why the work fits the journal's scope
6. **Suggested reviewers / conflicts** — Optional, only if requested
7. **Closing**

## Tone

- Professional and concise
- No overclaim (use "suggest", "indicate", "demonstrate" as appropriate)
- No detailed methods or results repetition

## Template

```text
[Date]

Editorial Office
[Journal Name]

Dear Editor,

We wish to submit our manuscript entitled "[Title]" for consideration as a [article type] in [Journal Name].

[One-paragraph summary: background, knowledge gap, approach, main finding, and implication.]

We believe this work aligns with the scope of [Journal Name] because [specific fit].

We declare no conflicts of interest. We suggest the following reviewers: [names and emails, optional].

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
[Corresponding author name and affiliation]
```

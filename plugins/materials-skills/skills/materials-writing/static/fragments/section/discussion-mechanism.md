# Discussion: Mechanism Interpretation

## Sentence patterns

Use these patterns to connect a result to a mechanism:

- "The increase in [response] may be attributed to [mechanism]."
- "This trend is consistent with [characterization evidence], suggesting that [mechanism]."
- "The observed [response] indicates that [mechanism] plays a dominant role."
- "The improvement in [response] is likely due to [mechanism], as evidenced by [evidence]."

## Addressing rival explanations

Before generalizing, name at least one alternative explanation and explain why it is less likely:

- "An alternative explanation is [rival mechanism]. However, [evidence] makes this less likely because [reason]."
- "While [confounding factor] could also contribute to [response], its effect is expected to be minor under [condition]."

## Boundary language

State where the interpretation stops holding:

- "This interpretation is valid within the [factor] range of [lower] to [upper]."
- "Beyond [condition], the mechanism may shift because [reason]."

## Evidence check

Before finalizing the Discussion, verify that every mechanism claim is supported by an entry in `experiment-record.yaml` → `characterization` or `evidence_links`.

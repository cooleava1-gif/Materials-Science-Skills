# Review Paper

Use this for a small review, mini-review, or thematic literature synthesis.

## Drafting order

Review papers should be organized by synthesis axes, not by paper-by-paper summaries:

1. **Synthesis axes** — define the thematic dimensions that organize the field (e.g., mechanism, material design, performance trade-off, research agenda).
2. **Gap** — identify the knowledge gap that blocks better material design.
3. **Trend** — describe how understanding has evolved along each axis.
4. **Outlook** — propose a research agenda that a reviewer can recognize as useful.

## Acceptable evidence for this paper type

- Synthesis of multiple studies, not isolated paper summaries.
- Thematic axes that are justified by the literature, not imposed for convenience.
- Explicit evidence gaps in the literature.
- Comparison of test methods, conditions, and material systems across studies.

## Synthesis axes (choose and justify)

Organize the review by axes such as:

- **Mechanism** — how the modification works (curing, crosslinking, interfacial bonding).
- **Material design** — formulation variables, modifiers, and processing routes.
- **Performance trade-off** — gain in one property vs. loss in another.
- **Research agenda** — what experiments must resolve next.

Do not default to a paper-by-paper annotated bibliography.

## Recommended taxonomy for waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt

- material components,
- emulsification and curing behavior,
- bonding-performance tests,
- durability and service conditioning,
- characterization evidence,
- construction workability,
- standards and field gaps.

The research agenda should identify what future experiments must resolve, such as dosage optimization under wet conditions, mechanism-performance coupling, and standard-compatible tack coat evaluation.

## Scope rule

For a master's-stage mini-review, keep the scope narrow enough to be deep: for example, waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt tack coat bonding and durability rather than all modified asphalt emulsions.

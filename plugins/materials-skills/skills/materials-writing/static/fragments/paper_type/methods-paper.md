# Methods Paper

Use this when the contribution is a protocol, workflow, testing method, or repeatable data-processing method.

## Drafting order

Methods papers prioritize reproducibility and validation:

1. **Protocol** — state the complete, repeatable procedure.
2. **Validation** — show that the method produces reliable, accurate, or comparable results.
3. **Boundary** — state the conditions, materials, and problem instances where the method applies and where it does not.

## Acceptable evidence for this paper type

- Full protocol detail: material source and specification, specimen preparation, environmental condition, loading or measurement protocol, data processing, uncertainty and repeatability.
- Validation data: benchmark comparison, repeatability across operators or days, detection limits, sensitivity analysis.
- Explicit method limitations, including materials or conditions the method has not been validated for.

## Required reporting blocks

- Material source and specification.
- Specimen or sample preparation.
- Environmental condition during preparation and testing.
- Loading or measurement protocol.
- Data processing and any software used.
- Uncertainty and repeatability estimate.
- Method limitations.

## Scope rule

Do not frame a routine test as a new method unless the procedure or validation is genuinely novel. A methods paper must add reproducibility value beyond a standard protocol citation.

## Overclaim warning

Avoid claiming broad applicability beyond the validated material systems, geometries, or conditions. Use "validated for ..." rather than "suitable for all ...".

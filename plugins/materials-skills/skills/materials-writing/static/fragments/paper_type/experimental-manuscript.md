# Experimental Manuscript

Use this for data-driven papers in civil materials, polymers, metals, ceramics, functional materials, nanomaterials, and related fields.

## Drafting order

Experimental manuscripts are easiest to control when drafted in this order:

1. **Results and Discussion** — establish what the data actually show.
2. **Introduction** — write the gap ladder that the Results fill.
3. **Conclusion** — state the contribution and boundaries once the evidence is fixed.
4. **Methods** — report the reproducible protocol behind the evidence.
5. **Abstract** — compress the full chain into one paragraph.

## Acceptable evidence for this paper type

- Quantitative performance data with units, replicates, and uncertainty.
- Mechanism evidence from at least one characterization technique; stronger with two or more cross-validating techniques.
- Comparison against a control, baseline, or literature value under matched conditions.
- Boundary conditions (test temperature, humidity, conditioning, scale) explicitly stated.

## Core argument chain

1. **Material problem** — current material has a performance or durability limitation.
2. **Design rationale** — modifier, dosage, or process is selected for a clear reason.
3. **test matrix** — control, modified groups, curing/conditioning, and mechanism tests.
4. **Performance result** — measured property improves under stated conditions.
5. **Mechanism evidence** — FTIR, SEM, fluorescence, rheology, XRD, TG/DTG, or other relevant evidence explains the trend.
6. **Boundary** — field durability or broad mechanism claims require additional validation.

## Section-level evidence rules

- **Introduction**: close with the evidence gap, not just the topic.
- **Methods**: report synthesis route, standards, replicates, and condition control.
- **Results**: keep claims near the data; do not discuss mechanism before the result.
- **Discussion**: address alternative explanations; state boundaries.
- **Conclusion**: block universal claims from bounded data.

For waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt, connect bonding performance to demulsification, curing, storage stability, viscosity, moisture conditioning, and interface morphology.

# Metals Language

Use metals-specific terminology:

- yield strength (Rp0.2 or 0.2% offset), not "strength" without qualifier,
- ultimate tensile strength (UTS), not "maximum strength",
- elongation at break or reduction of area, not "ductility" without measurement,
- hardness (HV, HRC, HB), not "hardness" without scale,
- grain size (ASTM E112 or intercept method), not "grain number",
- martensite start temperature (Ms), not "transformation temperature" without context,
- austenitizing, not "heating" for phase transformation treatment,
- tempering, not "annealing" after quench,
- precipitation hardening or age hardening, not "strengthening" without mechanism,
- dislocation density (TEM or XRD), not "defect density",
- fatigue life (S-N curve) or fatigue limit, not "endurance" without data,
- corrosion rate (mm/year or mpy), not "corrosion resistance" without quantification.

Do not describe heat treatment only as "annealing" without specifying temperature, time, atmosphere, and cooling rate. Report specimen orientation (LT, TL, ST) for fracture toughness and fatigue data.

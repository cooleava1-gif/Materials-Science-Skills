# Citation Integrity Rules

Every citation in a materials manuscript must be accurate, appropriate, and
traceable. This file defines the rules for attribution, verification, and
common citation errors.

---

## Four Attribution Types

| Type | When to use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct attribution** | Citing a specific finding from a specific study | `Kong et al. (2024) reported that WER modification increased bond strength by 45%.` |
| **Supporting attribution** | Citing a method, standard, or established procedure | `Specimens were prepared according to ASTM D7234.` |
| **Contextual attribution** | Citing background knowledge or general facts | `Emulsified asphalt is widely used in pavement maintenance (Zhang et al., 2017).` |
| **Comparative attribution** | Citing a contrasting or supporting result from another study | `Unlike the findings of Li et al. (2023), our results suggest a different optimum dosage.` |

## Citation Rules

### Rule 1: Read before you cite

Cite only sources you have personally read and verified. Do not cite a source
because another paper cited it (secondary citation). If you cannot access the
original, either find it or cite the secondary source directly.

### Rule 2: Cite the original study

Do not cite a review paper for a specific experimental finding. Cite the
original study that produced the data. Reviews are appropriate for:

- Background context
- Literature overview
- Established methods
- General knowledge synthesis

### Rule 3: Match claim to source

Do not cite a source for a claim it does not support. Common violations:

| Violuation | Example | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Citing a different material system | Citing a polymer study for asphalt claims | Cite the asphalt study |
| Citing a different test method | Citing tensile strength data for bond strength claims | Cite the bond strength study |
| Citing a different condition | Citing high-temperature data for room-temperature claims | Cite the room-temperature study |
| Citing conclusions without data | Citing a review's conclusion as if it were primary data | Cite the primary study |

### Rule 4: Verify page numbers and figures

If you cite a specific figure, table, or page number, verify it is correct.
Common errors:

- Wrong figure number (Fig. 3 vs. Fig. 4)
- Wrong table number
- Wrong page range in a multi-page paper
- Citing supplementary material as main text

### Rule 5: Reasonable citation density

| Section | Recommended density | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | 3–5 per paragraph | Heavy citation for context |
| Methods | 1–3 per paragraph | Standards and established methods |
| Results | 0–1 per paragraph | Results are your own data |
| Discussion | 2–3 per paragraph | Comparison with literature |
| Conclusions | 0–1 per paragraph | Usually no new citations |

### Rule 6: Citation format consistency

Use one citation style throughout:

| Style | Format | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| Author-year | `Kong et al. (2024)` | Most materials journals |
| Numbered | `[1]`, `[2–5]` | Some Elsevier journals |
| Superscript | `reported¹` | Rare in materials; check journal |

## Common Citation Errors

### 1. Review paper for specific finding

Wrong:
> WER modification increased bond strength by 45% [Review paper, 2023].

Right:
> WER modification increased bond strength by 45% (Kong et al., 2024).

### 2. Secondary citation

Wrong:
> Epoxy curing occurs at room temperature (Li et al., 2023, citing Wang et al., 2020).

Right:
> Epoxy curing occurs at room temperature (Wang et al., 2020).

If you cannot access Wang et al., cite Li et al. directly:
> Li et al. (2023) reported that epoxy curing occurs at room temperature.

### 3. Wrong scope

Wrong:
> The material showed excellent durability (Zhang et al., 2017).

If Zhang et al. only tested moisture resistance, not full durability:
> The material showed improved moisture resistance (Zhang et al., 2017).

### 4. Over-citing

Wrong:
> Emulsified asphalt is a commonly used material in road engineering [1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8].

Right:
> Emulsified asphalt is widely used in pavement maintenance (Zhang et al., 2017; Li et al., 2020).

### 5. Under-citing

Wrong:
> Previous studies have shown that WER modification improves bond strength.

Right:
> Previous studies have shown that WER modification improves bond strength (Kong et al., 2024; Li et al., 2023).

## Citation Audit Checklist

For each citation in the manuscript:

- [ ] I have personally read this source.
- [ ] The source supports the specific claim made.
- [ ] The citation type (direct, supporting, contextual, comparative) is appropriate.
- [ ] Page numbers, figures, and tables are correct.
- [ ] The citation is not a review paper for a specific experimental finding.
- [ ] The citation format is consistent with the rest of the manuscript.
- [ ] The citation density is reasonable for this section.
- [ ] No secondary citation is used when the original is accessible.

## Polishing Citation Language

When integrating citations into prose, use these patterns:

### Direct attribution

> Kong et al. (2024) reported that WER modification increased bond strength
> by 45% under dry conditions.

### Parenthetical

> WER modification has been shown to improve bond strength (Kong et al., 2024;
> Li et al., 2023).

### Comparative

> Unlike the findings of Li et al. (2023), who used a different curing agent,
> our system showed a broader curing window.

### Method citation

> Specimens were prepared according to ASTM D7234 and cured at 60°C for 24 h.

### Literature synthesis

> Several studies have investigated WER-asphalt systems, with bond strength
> improvements ranging from 30% to 73% depending on dosage and curing
> conditions (Kong et al., 2024; Li et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2017).

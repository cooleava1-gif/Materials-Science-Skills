# Abstract Polishing

> **Domain context**: The `domain` axis has loaded domain-specific polishing guidance for [detected domain]. The abstract must satisfy the domain's evidence standards — supplement this template with the domain guide.

Use a six-part logic:

1. Engineering or scientific context.
2. Specific gap.
3. Material strategy.
4. Methods.
5. Key quantified results.
6. Bounded implication.

Do not open with broad infrastructure slogans unless the next sentence narrows quickly.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Overbroad opening (civil)

Before:
> Road construction is very important for national development, and emulsified asphalt has important significance in pavement engineering.

After:
> Emulsified asphalt tack coats are widely used to improve interlayer bonding in asphalt pavements, but their early-age strength and moisture resistance remain sensitive to curing and formulation conditions.

Why: the revision replaces a broad slogan with a specific material, function, and unresolved limitation.

### Example 2: Overbroad opening (ceramics)

Before:
> Ceramic materials are widely used in many industries because of their excellent properties.

After:
> Alumina ceramics offer high hardness and thermal stability, but their application in [specific context] is limited by the trade-off between density and fracture toughness at sintering temperatures below 1600°C.

Why: replaces generic praise with a specific material class and a measurable processing-property bottleneck.

### Example 3: Unsupported mechanism (polymers)

Before:
> The composite showed excellent mechanical properties due to good compatibility.

After:
> The composite containing 5 wt% surface-treated filler showed a 30% increase in tensile modulus with only 8% reduction in elongation at break, suggesting improved interfacial load transfer with maintained ductility.

Why: replaces vague "good compatibility" with quantified performance and a bounded mechanism statement.

### Example 4: Vague result (metals)

Before:
> The heat treatment significantly improved the strength of the alloy.

After:
> After T6 heat treatment (530°C solution + 175°C aging for 8 h), the alloy achieved a yield strength of 350 MPa and UTS of 480 MPa, with 12% elongation — a 40% strength improvement over the as-cast condition with acceptable ductility retention.

Why: replaces "significant improvement" with specific processing parameters and quantified mechanical properties.

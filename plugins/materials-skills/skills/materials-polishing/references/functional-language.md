# Functional Materials Language

Use functional-materials-specific terminology:

- dielectric constant (εr) or relative permittivity, not "permittivity" without qualifier,
- loss tangent (tan δ) or dielectric loss, not "loss" without context,
- piezoelectric coefficient (d₃₃, d₃₁), not "piezoelectric response" without quantification,
- coercive field (Ec), not "switching field" for ferroelectrics,
- remanent polarization (Pr), not "residual polarization",
- Curie temperature (Tc), not "transition temperature" without specifying material class,
- impedance spectroscopy (Nyquist plot), not "resistance measurement" without frequency range,
- grain resistance (Rg) and grain-boundary resistance (Rgb), not "total resistance",
- activation energy (Ea) from Arrhenius plot, not "energy barrier" without data,
- band gap (Eg) via Tauc plot, not "optical gap" without specifying direct/indirect,
- carrier concentration and mobility (Hall effect), not "conductivity" without transport parameters,
- sheet resistance (Ω/sq), not "resistivity" for thin films,
- Seebeck coefficient (µV/K), not "thermoelectric performance" without ZT.

Do not describe dielectric properties only as "high permittivity" without reporting frequency, temperature, and field conditions. Report poling conditions (field, temperature, time) alongside piezoelectric coefficient claims.

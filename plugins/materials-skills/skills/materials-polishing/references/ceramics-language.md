# Ceramics Language

Use ceramics-specific terminology:

- sintering temperature, not "firing temperature",
- grain size or grain growth, not "particle size" (after sintering),
- porosity (open/closed/total), not "voids",
- intergranular phase, not "grain boundary layer",
- fracture toughness (K_IC), not "toughness" without qualifier,
- flexural strength (3-point or 4-point), not "bending strength" in formal text,
- Weibull modulus, not "reliability index",
- thermal shock resistance, not "heat resistance",
- ionic conductivity or electronic conductivity, not "conductivity" without qualifier,
- phase purity (XRD Rietveld), not "crystal quality",
- densification or relative density, not "compaction",
- grain boundary resistance (EIS), not "resistance" without context.

Do not describe ceramic processing only as "sintering" without reporting temperature, atmosphere, ramp rate, and dwell time. Report Weibull statistics when strength variability is discussed.

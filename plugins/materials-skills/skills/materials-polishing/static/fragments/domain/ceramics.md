# Ceramics — Polishing Guide

## Terminology to verify

| Term | Domain-specific check |
|---|---|
| density | Relative or absolute? Theoretical density reference must be stated |
| sintering | Must include temperature + ramp rate + dwell + atmosphere |
| Weibull modulus | Minimum 10 specimens (preferably 30) for statistical validity |
| toughness | KIC measurement method must be stated (SENB, SEPB, IF, CNB) |
| phase | XRD identification requires reference pattern matching or Rietveld |

## Common overclaim patterns

| Overclaim | Safer alternative |
|---|---|
| "Dense ceramics were obtained" | "Relative density of [value]% was achieved at [sintering T]" |
| "The ceramic has high toughness" | "Fracture toughness of [value] MPa·m^(1/2) was measured by [method]" |
| "The material is bioactive" | "HA formation was observed on the surface after [days] in SBF" |
| "Excellent thermal stability" | "No phase transformation was detected up to [T]°C by XRD/DSC" |

## Hedging guide

| Evidence level | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| XRD + Rietveld + multiple samples | confirms, demonstrates | "XRD with Rietveld refinement confirms the [phase] content of [X]%" |
| SEM + EDS without statistics | suggests, is consistent with | "SEM suggests a uniform grain size distribution" |
| Single property measurement | may indicate, could reflect | "The increased hardness may indicate successful densification" |

## Journal-specific language

- Always report "relative density (% of theoretical)" before mechanical properties.
- For bioceramics: "bioactivity" requires SBF test per ISO 23317 or cell assay.
- "High temperature" is vague: specify "up to [value]°C in [atmosphere]".

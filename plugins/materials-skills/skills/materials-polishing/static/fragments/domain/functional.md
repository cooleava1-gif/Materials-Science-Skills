# Functional / Electronic Materials — Polishing Guide

## Terminology to verify

| Term | Domain-specific check |
|---|---|
| bandgap | Direct or indirect? Tauc plot method must be stated |
| efficiency | PCE, EQE, or internal? Active area must be specified |
| stability | "Stable" requires defined test duration and retention percentage |
| conductivity | Electronic or ionic? Four-probe or two-probe? Temperature? |
| mobility | Hall effect or FET? Units: cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹ |

## Common overclaim patterns

| Overclaim | Safer alternative |
|---|---|
| "High efficiency was achieved" | "A PCE of [X]% was measured under [illumination] (active area: [value])" |
| "The material has excellent stability" | "[X]% of initial efficiency was retained after [duration] under [condition]" |
| "Defect-free material" | "No obvious defects were detected by TEM/XRD within the instrument resolution" |
| "Commercial potential" | Requires cost analysis or scalability data, not just performance |

## Hedging guide

| Evidence level | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Multiple devices + statistics | demonstrates, achieves | "The device demonstrates a reproducible PCE of [X]% ± [Y]% (n = [Z])" |
| Single best device | exhibits, shows | "The best device exhibits a PCE of [X]%" |
| Preliminary data | suggests, may indicate | "CV suggests a reversible redox process at [potential]" |

## Journal-specific language

- Report measurement conditions: "AM 1.5G illumination" not "standard light".
- Distinguish "active area" from "device area".
- For batteries: report C-rate, voltage window, and electrolyte composition.
- "High performance" requires benchmark comparison with state-of-the-art.

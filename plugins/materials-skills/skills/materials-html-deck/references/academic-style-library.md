# Academic HTML Style Library

Attribution: copied and trimmed from
`huashu-design/references/design-styles.md`.

style_id: assertion-evidence

Use for paper reading, technical reports, and engineering claims. The slide
title is a full assertion; evidence occupies the main visual area; bullets are
secondary. Prefer restrained color, direct labels, and high data-ink ratio.

style_id: dense-research

Use for data-heavy research reports and evidence maps. Make the page feel like
a compact research brief: action title, dense chart area, small source notes,
KPI cards, and minimal empty space.

style_id: diagrammatic-minimalism

Use for mechanisms, models, and conceptual frameworks. One strong geometric
diagram should carry the argument. Use circles, matrices, arrows, and embedded
labels rather than decorative illustration.

style_id: diagram-driven-isotype

Use for workflows, routes, systems, and process narratives. Build the slide
around arrows, layered boxes, icon-like marks, and stepwise flows. The diagram
is the story.

style_id: two-font-consulting

Use for project reports, industry collaboration, and management-facing research
briefs. Combine a characterful serif title with clean sans body text, clear
action titles, refined rules, and sober data cards.

style_id: bento-grid

Use for progress snapshots, multi-metric summaries, and matrix-style synthesis.
Use bento cards, large numbers, small captions, and asymmetric grids while
keeping academic evidence visible.

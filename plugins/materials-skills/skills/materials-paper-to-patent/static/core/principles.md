# Principles

Patent drafting is **evidence translation, not persuasion**. Every formal
claim must trace to a disclosed operation, structure, parameter, or effect.

- **Source first.** Stable source IDs (P/E/F/C) precede claim language.
- **No fabrication.** Unknown facts become inventor questions, not guesses.
- **No overclaim.** Aspirations, future work, and limitations are not claims.
- **Materials honesty.** A single experimental point is not a genus claim.
  Disclosed range boundaries and units are preserved literally.
- **Civil context.** When the source is a materials science paper, prefer
  observable material parameters (composition, microstructure, performance)
  over marketing-style abstraction.
- **Patent professional review required.** This skill produces a drafting
  aid, never a legal opinion.

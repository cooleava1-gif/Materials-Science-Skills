# Nano Domain Citation Rules

For nano and nanomaterials manuscripts, keep evidence separated by these layers:

- **Synthesis/Preparation**: precursor, method (sol-gel, hydrothermal, CVD, PVD, ball milling, exfoliation), temperature, atmosphere, duration.
- **Size/Morphology**: particle size, size distribution, aspect ratio, surface area (BET), shape (sphere, rod, tube, sheet, platelet).
- **Surface Chemistry**: capping agent, functionalization, surface charge (zeta potential), hydrophilicity/hydrophobicity.
- **Dispersion**: dispersion method (ultrasonication, high-shear mixing), solvent/matrix, agglomeration state (TEM/DLS evidence).
- **Characterization**: XRD (crystal structure), TEM/SEM (morphology), FTIR/Raman (functional groups), XPS (surface composition), UV-Vis (optical properties).
- **Performance**: mechanical, thermal, optical, electrical, catalytic, or biological response.

Separate synthesis-condition citations from property citations. Particle size and size distribution must be reported with measurement method (TEM, DLS, XRD Scherrer) before claiming size-dependent properties.

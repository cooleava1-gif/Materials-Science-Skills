# Thermal Insulation Domain Citation Rules

For thermal insulation manuscripts, keep evidence separated by these layers:

- **Composition/Structure**: aerogel matrix, fiber type, pore-former, chemical composition, density.
- **Thermal performance**: thermal conductivity (λ), R-value, heat flux, steady-state vs transient measurement.
- **Microstructure**: pore size distribution, porosity, SEM/TEM, BET surface area.
- **Mechanical**: compressive strength, flexural strength, elastic modulus, creep.
- **Durability**: hydrothermal aging, UV exposure, freeze-thaw, cyclic heating, service-life prediction.
- **Fire performance**: LOI, cone calorimetry, flame spread, smoke density, UL94 rating.
- **Hygrothermal**: moisture sorption, water vapor permeability, capillary absorption.

Separate thermal-performance citations from fire-safety citations. Measurement method (hot plate, hot wire, laser flash, heat flow meter) must be stated before comparing conductivity values across studies.

# Metals Domain Citation Rules

For metals and alloys manuscripts, keep evidence separated by these layers:

- **Composition/Alloy design**: alloy grade, composition (wt% or at%), trace elements, standard designation (AISI, ASTM, EN, GB).
- **Processing**: casting, rolling, forging, extrusion, heat treatment (austenitizing, quenching, tempering, annealing), welding parameters.
- **Microstructure**: grain size, phase fraction, precipitate size/distribution, dislocation density, SEM/TEM/EBSD evidence.
- **Mechanical properties**: tensile, yield, elongation, hardness, impact toughness, fatigue, creep.
- **Corrosion/Durability**: corrosion rate, pitting potential, passivation behavior, oxidation resistance, environment.
- **Fracture**: fracture toughness (K_IC, J_IC), crack growth rate (da/dN), fractography (SEM).

Separate processing-microstructure citations from property citations. Heat treatment parameters (temperature, time, cooling rate) must be reported before claiming microstructure-property relationships.
